<p><a href="http://twitter.com/evanphx">Evan Phoenix</a> has announced that the <a href="http://rubini.us/">Rubinius</a> project has hit a major milestone: <a href="http://blog.fallingsnow.net/2008/05/17/rails-on-rubinius/">Rubinius can run Rails!</a> This makes it implementation #3 (after MRI and JRuby) to join the Rails club and will help cement its reputation as a strong, key implementation to watch in the future. Chad Fowler goes as far as to <a href="http://www.chadfowler.com/2008/5/17/ruby-on-rails-on-rubinius">assert</a> that in a year's time, Rubinius will be used in production deployments and quickly become the defacto standard Ruby implementation shortly thereafter.</p>
<p>Eyes are now on Microsoft's implementation, <a href="http://www.ironruby.net/">IronRuby</a>, that may also be joining the Rails club soon.</p>
