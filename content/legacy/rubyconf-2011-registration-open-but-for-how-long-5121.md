<p><strong>Quick, quick, <a href="http://rubyconf.org/">RubyConf 2011</a> registration has opened today and <strike>until they run out, you can <a href="http://rubyconf.stagehq.com/">buy tickets at this link.</a></strike></strong> (They cost $350.) <strong>(Update July 15 - they seem to have sold out!)</strong> Why the urgency? RubyConf is notorious for selling out quickly (I think RubyConf 2009 sold out in 5 hours or something crazy?) and there are still tickets left now, 9 hours later.</p>
<p><strong>RubyConf is taking place between September 29 - October 1, 2011</strong> and it's in New Orleans, Louisiana, as last year, at the Astor Crowne Plaza Hotel in the French Quarter.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://rubyconf.org/schedule">schedule has also been posted</a> and its highlights include sessions like:</p>
<ul>
<li>Exceptional Ruby by Avdi Grimm</li>
<li>Writing Solid Ruby Code by Jim Weirich</li>
<li>Advanced EventMachine by Jonathan Weiss</li>
<li>GitHub Flavored Ruby by Tom Preston-Werner</li>
<li>Ruby in the browser with NativeClient (NaCl) by Ilya Grigorik</li>
<li>.. and about 55 other sessions from luminaries like Jamis Buck, David A Black, Steve Klabnik, Sarah Mei, and too many others to mention.</li>
</ul>
<p>So not much more to say here, if you want to go, <a href="http://rubyconf.org/">go now before it sells out.</a></p>
<p><em>(Update: It looks like they sold out. Hope you got in!)</em></p>
