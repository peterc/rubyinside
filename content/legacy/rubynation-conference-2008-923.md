<p><a href="http://www.rubynation.org/">RubyNation</a> is a new Ruby conference launching August 1 &amp; 2, 2008. It <a href="http://www.rubynation.org/about">bills itself</a> as an annual Ruby conference serving the Virginia, West Virginia, Maryland, and Washington DC areas. It costs $175 to register and you get admission for both days, lunches, snacks, drinks and a conference t-shirt.</p>
<p>Of particular note is the level of speakers the organizers have managed to obtain to such a new, locally organized conference. Neal Ford, Stuart Halloway, Rich Kilmer, David Bock, <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/">Giles Bowkett</a>, Yehuda Katz, David Keener, Russ Olsen, Bruce Tate, and Glenn Vanderburg <a href="http://www.rubynation.org/speakers">are all confirmed speakers</a> so far.</p>
<p><small>As an aside, and not to detract from RubyNation, why aren't there more multi-day Ruby / Rails events that don't have hours of scheduled sessions, like <a href="http://wiki.railscamp.net/w/show/HomePage">RailsCamp</a>?</small></p>
