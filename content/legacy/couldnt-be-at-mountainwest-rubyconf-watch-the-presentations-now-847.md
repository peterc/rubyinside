<p>If you couldn't get to the popular <a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf.org/">MountainWest RubyConf</a> two weeks ago, don't fear. In conjunction with Confreaks, <a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/">videos of all of the presentations are available to watch</a> online. 14 videos are available, including the following:</p>
<div style="margin-left: 1em">
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/01phoenix.html"><strong>Strengthening the Ruby Ecosystem Part I: Rubinius</strong></a> <strong>by Evan Phoenix</strong> - Evan gives a general overview of Rubinius, how the project has worked out so far, and what the goal is with its development.</p>
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/02zygmuntowicz.html"><strong>Strengthening the Ruby Ecosystem Part II: Merb</strong></a> <strong>by Ezra Zygmuntowicz</strong> - A similarly general overview of Merb, a Ruby Web applications framework. A great executive overview of the Merb project generally.</p>
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/03bowkett.html"><strong>Code Generation: The Safety Scissors of Metaprogramming</strong></a> <strong>by Giles Bowkett</strong> - Giles looks at the beauties (and dangers!) of meta programming and other voodoo with Ruby. This is a particularly interesting talk, and an ideal one to watch if you're short of time.</p>
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/04katz.html"><strong>Faster, Better ORM with DataMapper</strong></a> <strong>by Yehuda Katz</strong> - An overview of the <a href="http://datamapper.org/">DataMapper</a> ORM, a compelling alternative to ActiveRecord.</p>
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/07mcanally.html"><strong>Deep Ruby</strong></a> <strong>by Jeremy McAnally</strong> - A look at Ruby's object model and other "deep" Ruby issues. This is a pretty interesting talk too, for those who like to look at the nitty gritty.</p>
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/12saleh.html"><strong>BDD with Shoulda</strong></a> <strong>by Tammer Saleh</strong> - <a href="http://www.thoughtbot.com/projects/shoulda">Shoulda</a> is a great, small, simple testing plugin for Rails applications with a BDD flavor.</p>
<p><a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf2008.confreaks.com/06lightning.html"><strong>Lightning Talks</strong></a> <strong>by Various</strong> - The lightning talks include Mike Moore doing a "Binary Lottery" program with _why's Shoes, Chris Shea talking about "<a href="http://guessmethod.rubyforge.org/">Guessmethod</a>", Ed Moss talking about <a href="http://activescaffold.com/">Active Scaffold</a>, and more.</p>
</div>
<p>It seems MountainWest had a really high quality level of talks, so give it a thought when choosing which Ruby events to attend next year.</p>
<p><em><small>(On another level, I'm kinda surprised by how many Rubyists seem to have ended up working in groups - whether ThoughtBot, ENTP, or Engine Yard.)</small></em></p>
