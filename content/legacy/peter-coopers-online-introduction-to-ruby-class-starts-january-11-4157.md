<p><img src="/assets/2011/01/codelesson.png" width="124" height="50" alt="codelesson.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px; border:1px #000000 solid;">Starting January 10, 2011 (just one week after this post), <strong>I'll be teaching an <a href="http://codelesson.com/ruby">online Ruby course</a></strong> with <i>CodeLesson.com</i> - me being <a href="http://peterc.org/">Peter Cooper</a>, author of <a href="http://beginningruby.org/"><i>Beginning Ruby</i></a>. It lasts 4 weeks and will cover most things a new Ruby developer (or programmer in general) needs to know to become a confident, intermediate-level Rubyist. It's a great springboard from which to move on to Rails or other more advanced Ruby topics.</p>
<p>Naturally, the course is <i>below</i> the experience level of most Ruby Inside readers, but it might suit your boss, your new non-Ruby co-workers, or merely anyone you know who has some tech savvy and would find learning programming in Ruby a buzz.</p>
<p>Topics I'll be covering include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Installing and running Ruby</li>
<li>Using the interactive Ruby shell (irb)</li>
<li>Looking up Ruby documentation and help</li>
<li>Expressions and variables</li>
<li>Built-in data types (strings, arrays, hashes, etc.)</li>
<li>Flow control</li>
<li>Classes and modules</li>
<li>RubyGems and library management</li>
<li>File handling and IO</li>
<li>Web and network access</li>
<li>Producing documentation</li>
<li>Testing</li>
<li>The Ruby community and how to benefit from it</li>
<li>Ruby project structure</li>
<li>Rack and Sinatra (to produce basic webapps)</li>
<li>Building and releasing a gem</li>
<li>RSpec</li>
</ul>
<p>Except for the topics covered each week, the course isn't tightly scheduled. Participants don't need to be online certain times or follow along with real-time "webinar" presentations. It's more like a distance learning course. There'll be instructions to follow, articles to read, videos to watch, and a course textbook to refer to (my book <a href="http://beginningruby.org/">Beginning Ruby</a> - a free copy is provided). Questions and discussion then take place on forums provided by CodeLesson.</p>
<p>Nonetheless, I'm going to also run some optional "surgery hours" where people can come to me on IRC or instant messaging to ask me questions or get a walk through any tricky topics. The timing of these will be determined as to the participants' various needs as we go along.</p>
<p>The course costs $295 for the 4 weeks (though there's a discount for group signups) and the main value comes from having access to me, asking me questions, and letting me walk you through particular sticking points. You can pick up a book for $30 but a course like this gives you a mentor, a schedule, and everything I provide will be well suited to learning Ruby at a level suitable for 2011 - a tricky promise for any book to offer given how quickly the Ruby world changes.</p>
<p>So, interested? Or have a boss/coworker/similar who'd benefit? Check out the <a href="http://codelesson.com/ruby">Ruby course page on CodeLesson.com</a> for more info. If you join the course, I'll see you next week!</p>
<p><i>P.S. If you have any questions, I can be found at.. peter -at- peterc.org.</i></p>
