<p>Peter Armstrong has just released the initial version of his PDF-only book, <a href="http://www.flexiblerails.com/">Flexible Rails</a>. It's available for $20, and anyone who buys the book now will get free PDF copies of every subsequent version of the first edition of the book. You can also order the book in 5 copy, 10 copy, and 30 copy editions for use in the workplace.</p>
<p>The book is at 214 pages and Peter claims is only 20-40% complete so far. Despite this, the current table of contents includes:</p>
<blockquote><p><em>Overviews of Rails 1.1, Flex 2, Flash 9, and how they all work together<br>
How to install everything<br>
Creating an initial 'hello world' project<br>
Flex login systems<br>
Sending XML with HTTPService<br>
Creating a basic GUI with MXML<br>
Getting tasks to show up in the task grid<br>
Debugging XML<br>
Refactoring</em></p></blockquote>
<p>Looks like it could become <em>the</em> guide for those interested in the interoperability of Rails and Flex. <a href="http://www.flexiblerails.com/">Check it out</a>.</p>
