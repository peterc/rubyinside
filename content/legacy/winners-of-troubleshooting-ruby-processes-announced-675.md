<p>Two weeks ago <a href="3-copies-of-troubleshooting-ruby-processes-to-win-664.html">we ran a contest</a> where you could talk about the sort of Ruby books you'd like to see available and have a chance to win one of three copies of "<a href="3-copies-of-troubleshooting-ruby-processes-to-win-664.html">Troubleshooting Ruby Processes</a>" by Philippe Hanrigou. The three winners are <span style="font-style: italic;">CptOatmeal</span>, <span style="font-style: italic;">Andrew Simard</span>, and <span style="font-style: italic;">Raju Gandhi</span> who will each receive a copy via e-mail from the publisher shortly. I'd like to thank everyone who commented.. there were 56 comments in all and tons of amazing book ideas. I wouldn't be surprised if you saw the publishers announcing books along the lines of the suggestions soon!</p>
<p>Staying on the topic of Troubleshooting Ruby Processes, author <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/12/philippe-hanrigou-interview-and-more.html">Philippe Hanrigou was recently interviewed</a> by Pat Eyler of On Ruby. They went into depth on topics like DTrace and debugging JRuby applications. Pat also wrote his <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/12/philippe-hanrigou-has-written-excellent.html">own review of the book</a>.</p>
