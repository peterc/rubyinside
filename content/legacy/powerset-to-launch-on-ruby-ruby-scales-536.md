<p><img src="/assets/2007/06/powerset.jpg" height="198" width="156" border="1" hspace="4" vspace="4" alt="Powerset" title="powerset.png"></p>
<p>Kevin Clark revels that <a href="http://www.powerset.com/">Powerset</a> - a "natural language" based search engine, seen by some as a future "Google killer" - is <a href="http://glu.ttono.us/articles/2007/06/21/powerset-to-launch-front-end-on-ruby">going to launch with a Ruby powered front end</a>. In itself, this isn't particularly amazing, although if Powerset realizes its Google-killing goals, having a major search engine powered by Ruby will be kudos indeed.</p>
<p>Of more interest is that Powerset is using a mixture of Rails, <a href="http://merb.devjavu.com/">Merb</a>, and <a href="http://camping.rubyforge.org/files/README.html">Camping</a> apps throughout the company, and that Powerset is "not worried about scaling." Clark quotes Blaine Cook of <a href="http://www.twitter.com/">Twitter</a>:</p>
<blockquote><p>Rails and Ruby haven’t been stumbling blocks, compared to any other language or framework. The performance boosts associated with a “faster” language would give us a 10-20% improvement, but thanks to architectural changes that Ruby and Rails happily accommodated, Twitter is 10000% faster than it was in January.</p></blockquote>
<p>Clark then looks at what all of this means for the Ruby community.. and for that, you need to go check out <a href="http://glu.ttono.us/articles/2007/06/21/powerset-to-launch-front-end-on-ruby">his post</a>.</p>
