<p><a href="http://www.nomedojogo.com/livro/carlosbrando-rubyonrails21_en.pdf">Ruby on Rails 2.1: What's New?</a> (<span style="font-style: italic;">PDF, 2MB</span>) is a <a href="http://www.nomedojogo.com/2008/06/09/new-free-book-ruby-on-rails-21-whats-new/">free e-book</a> written by <a href="http://www.nomedojogo.com/">Carlos Brando</a> (with help from Marcos Tapajos and Daniel Lopes). It was initially written in Brazilian Portuguese (<a href="http://www.nomedojogo.com/2008/06/06/o-primeiro-livro-sobre-rails-21-e-brasileiro/">find the original version here</a>), but this version is an English translation by several community members.</p>
<p>As far as community written books goes, it's pretty good! It comes in at 124 pages, and while 30 of these are just the Rails 2.1 CHANGELOGs (formatted in a nice way, none the less), the rest definitely does a good job of presenting some of the new features of Rails 2.1 (such as named scopes, dirty objects, numerous ActiveRecord changes and time related functions) in a very easy to digest manner.</p>
