<p><img src="/assets/2007/05/scaling.jpg" height="123" width="271" border="1" hspace="4" vspace="4" alt="Scaling"></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://joyeur.com/2007/05/18/slides-for-my-railsconf-talk-scaling-a-rails-application-from-the-bottom-up">Scaling a Rails Application from the Bottom Up</a></strong><strong> </strong>was a presentation by Jason Hoffman, CTO of <a href="http://www.joyent.com/">Joyent</a>. The PDF of the presentation has a mega 192 slides and touches on a ton of interesting stuff about scaling and deployment (all the way down to hash based filing).</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2007/05/forkoff.jpg" height="193" width="271" border="1" hspace="4" vspace="4" alt="Forkoff"></p>
<p><strong>Angels &amp; Daemons</strong> (<a href="http://www.thoughtbot.com/assets/angels_and_daemons.pdf">PDF of slides</a>) was a presentation by Tammer Saleh of <a href="http://www.thoughtbot.com/">Thoughtbot, Inc</a> that went into the details of building daemons and how to daemonize processes in Ruby. The PDF on its own is pretty useful.<br>
<img src="/assets/2007/05/hcapistrano.jpg" height="157" width="268" border="1" hspace="4" vspace="4" alt="Hcapistrano"></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://presentations.jamisbuck.org/railsconf2007/">Harnessing Capistrano</a></strong> came from Jamis Buck of <a href="http://www.37signals.com/">37signals</a>. The presentation is reasonably heavy on practical knowledge and will be of use to Capistrano newcomers.</p>
