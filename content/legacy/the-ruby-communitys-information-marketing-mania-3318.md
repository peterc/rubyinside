<p><img src="/assets/2010/06/giles23.jpg" alt="" width="120" height="140" class="alignright size-full wp-image-3416" style="margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px; float: left; border: 1px solid #333">Hi - this is a guest post by Giles Bowkett. I made at least $10,000 in the last few months selling videos on my personal blog (including at least $5,000 selling <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2010/03/programmers-what-to-do-if-you-get-fired.html?utm_source=rubyinside&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=infomarketing_in_ruby_community">a video about how to have a terrific programming career</a>, and at least $5,000 selling <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2010/05/internet-marketing-for-alpha-geeks-re.html?utm_source=rubyinside&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=infomarketing_in_ruby_community2">a video about how to sell videos on your blog</a>).</p>
<p>As crazy and hucksterish as this might seem, I'm not the only one. <a href="http://peepcode.com/">PeepCode</a>, the <a href="http://www.pragprog.com/screencasts">Pragmatic Programmers</a>, <a href="http://envycasts.com/">RailsEnvy</a>, <a href="http://thinkcode.tv/">ThinkCode.tv</a>, and <a href="http://www.codeulatescreencasts.com/">Codeulate</a> all sell Ruby-related screencasts; Rubyists selling Ruby-related ebooks include <a href="http://www.railsupgradehandbook.com/">Jeremy McAnally</a>, former Rails Activist <a href="http://www.railsfreelancebook.com/">Mike</a> <a href="http://www.railsrescuebook.com/">Gunderloy</a>, <a href="http://8dc2fg6673tfx6f6ea1yt90uf1.hop.clickbank.net/">Marc-André Cournoyer</a>, the husband-and-wife team of <a href="http://javascriptrocks.com/performance/">Thomas Fuchs and Amy Hoy</a>, and, if you go back as far as 2006, <a href="http://37signals.com/svn/archives2/30day_update_getting_real_the_book.php">DHH himself, and 37Signals as a whole</a> (who made $120,000 in one month selling a PDF off their blog, and in the process sparked a debate with Tim O'Reilly about whether traditional publishers still had any value at all - a debate somewhat ironic in the context of this year's new, traditionally-published 37Signals book, <i>Rework</i>). Fuchs and Hoy also sell online training webinars, using a business model familiar to classic internet information marketing, but on topics like <a href="http://javascriptmasterclass.com/">JavaScript</a> and <a href="http://yearofhustle.com/">how to ship projects</a>.</p>
<p>Just this week, Michael Hartl of Rails Tutorial has created <a href="http://www.railstutorial.org/vanguard">an audacious, $495 webinar/screencasts program</a> as well. Meanwhile, Xavier Shay is touring the US with his <a href="http://www.dbisyourfriend.com/"><i>Database Is Your Friend</i></a> live training program, and of course the <a href="http://pragmaticstudio.com/"><i>Pragmatic Studio</i></a> live trainings have been going for years. (I attended the TDD studio twice, it's great.) Live training sometimes goes one-on-one, as well: I give programmers <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2009/12/blogs-are-godless-communist-bullshit.html">personal coaching</a>, as does <a href="http://blog.majesticseacreature.com/">Greg</a> <a href="http://twitter.com/seacreature/status/16268170528">Brown</a> (of Prawn fame). Somewhat atypically for this list, Greg is also developing a free program called <a href="http://blog.rubybestpractices.com/posts/gregory/026-ruby-mendicant-university-is-a-go.html">Ruby Mendicant University</a> which uses the info-marketing "webinar" model but gives everything away for free.</p>
<p>Although info marketing's been part of the Ruby community since 2006, it's grown in the past year; many of the projects listed above are new in the last six months. What does it mean? A rough, practical entrepreneurship runs strong in the Ruby and Rails communities, with a spirit audacious and independent enough to border on a punk rock vibe.</p>
<p>DHH has made fun of venture capitalists for spending millions to launch a company, when he built Basecamp on just ten hours a week, but ten hours a week is overkill for an information marketing business. I literally started my first such business with $30, selling Warcraft ebooks with pay-per-click advertizing. Rubyists are reacting to the dismal economy with their own gutsy ventures, which is what we should expect.</p>
