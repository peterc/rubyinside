<p><a href="http://apress.com/info/dailydeal"><img src="/assets/2008/06/beginning-ruby-cover.jpg" width="219" height="282" alt="beginning-ruby-cover.png" style="border:1px #000000 solid;"></a></p>
<p>I've had word from Apress that they're <strike>doing a one-off 24 hour <a href="http://apress.com/info/dailydeal">$10 sale on the e-book version of Beginning Ruby</a> as part of their "daily deal" series.</strike> <strong>The 24 hours is up - sorry!</strong> - but the <a href="http://apress.com/book/view/9781590597668">e-book is still available from Apress</a> at the usual rate of $27.99. It comes as a password protected PDF - no crazy DRM.</p>
<p><em>Beginning Ruby</em> (<a href="http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ruby-Novice-Professional/dp/1590597664/">Amazon link to the print version</a>) is the ideal book for those new to Ruby, whether fresh to programming or coming from other languages. As well as covering Ruby in a general sense, it also covers the community, a handful of interesting RubyGems, database programming &amp; SQL, object orientation (from the ground up), Rails (though very briefly), and network programming. Not convinced yet? The foreword was <em><strong>drawn</strong></em> by <em>Why The Lucky Stiff</em>, and of the <a href="http://www.amazon.com/review/product/1590597664/ref=dp_db_cm_cr_acr_txt?_encoding=UTF8&amp;showViewpoints=1">21 reviews of the book</a> on Amazon.com, 18 give it five stars, and 3 give it four stars (the reviews are well worth reading to see if it's the sort of book for you).</p>
<p>At $10, even if you're a bit beyond the level of Beginning Ruby, it might make a <strong>good purchase for a co-worker</strong>, family member, or anyone else who's hassling you about Ruby. Feel free to buy a copy and share it around the office. You're probably not meant to, but I don't care because if you really wanted to steal, you can just get it off of BitTorrent anyway :)</p>
<p>Finally, while on the Beginning Ruby theme, Amazon has <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ruby-Novice-Professional/dp/B0017UEQU2/ref=ed_oe_k">Beginning Ruby available for the Kindle</a>. It's $23.75, and appears to be the best selling Ruby book on the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kindle">Kindle</a> so far (which isn't saying a lot, I appreciate).</p>
