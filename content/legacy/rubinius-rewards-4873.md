<p><img src="/assets/2011/05/rubstickers.gif" alt="" title="rubstickers" width="120" height="120" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4875" style="border: 1px solid #666; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 10px; float: left">You've given <a href="http://rubini.us/">Rubinius</a> a spin, right? And contributed code to the project? If you didn't already know, Rubinius is an 'alternative' Ruby-sorta-written-in-Ruby implementation that's production ready and has been going from strength to strength recently (I <a href="rubinius-1-2-0-released-4103.html">post about it</a> quite a bit). And whatever your answers to those questions, <strong>the Rubinius team are kicking things up a notch by <em>bribing</em> you to get involved!</strong></p>
<h3>The Rubinius Rewards Program</h3>
<p>The Rubinius team has announced <a href="http://rubini.us/2011/05/26/rubinius-rewards/">the "Rubinius Rewards" program.</a> The good part is that they have lots of "general availability" stickers and t-shirts available for anyone who wants to write in and claim one. Send an e-mail, get a reward. Until they run out, of course.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2011/05/rubinius-tshirts.jpg" alt="" title="rubinius-tshirts" width="638" height="203" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4876"></p>
<p><em>If you want to donate financially to Rubinius, <a href="http://pledgie.com/campaigns/15386">they have a Pledgie campaign on the go</a> too.</em></p>
<h3>Special Stuff for Special People</h3>
<p>The <em>deeper</em> part of the announcement, though, is that they have some <em>special edition</em> stickers and t-shirts for people who contribute in more direct ways:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>First Commit Sticker</strong> - a sticker given to anyone who makes a single commit to the Rubinius project. Get a single commit into Rubinius and.. you're good to go.</li>
<li>
<strong>Tenth Commit Shirt</strong> - a committer-only t-shirt for people who've shown some dedication to the project by getting 10 commits into the project.</li>
<li>
<strong>Quarterly Committer Merit Badges</strong> - a quarterly sticker (marked with the quarter and year) sent to all committers during that quarter. Keeping ya hooked.</li>
</ul>
<p>If you've <em>already</em> committed to Rubinius and are hankering for a committer sticker, never fear. Just get in touch with the team as described in <a href="http://rubini.us/2011/05/26/rubinius-rewards/">the post</a> and they'll hook you up.</p>
<h3>C'mon.. It's Easy</h3>
<p>If you still haven't got on the Rubinius bandwagon but you're running RVM, there's seriously no excuse not to at least give it a quick spin on your current project. Just go <code>rvm install rbx-head</code> and you're on your way.</p>
<p>Want to go directly <a href="https://github.com/evanphx/rubinius">to the GitHub repository?</a> You can do that too at <a href="https://github.com/evanphx/rubinius">https://github.com/evanphx/rubinius</a></p>
<p>I'll be posting more about Rubinius, how to tinker with it, and how to get involved with the project over time, but perhaps a little bribery is enough to get some of you to give it a look :-)</p>
