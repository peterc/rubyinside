<p><a href="http://www.apress.com/book/bookDisplay.html?bID=10261">Practical Ruby Gems</a> is a new book written by David Berube and published by Apress that looks at the practical uses of a collection of Ruby gems. Tim Hunter, who also reviewed my <a href="http://books.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=07/04/23/1429230&amp;from=rss">Beginning Ruby</a> (which is doing a good job of tanking in the Amazon rankings, but I'm in it for the long haul!), has written <a href="http://books.slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=07/06/18/1623249">a lengthy review for Slashdot</a>. Here's a <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/1590598113/">direct affiliate-free link to the book on Amazon.com</a>. I was expecting to review a copy myself, but I'm more than happy to trust Tim based on his previous reviews of Ruby books.</p>
