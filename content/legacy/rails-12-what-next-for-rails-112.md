<p><a href="http://cwilliams.textdriven.com/">Chris Williams</a> looks at David Heinemeier Hansson's keynote speech at last weekend's RailsConf in Chicago and <a href="http://cwilliams.textdriven.com/articles/2006/06/26/railsconf-and-rails-1-2">examines what David has to say about Rails 1.2</a> (<em>scroll down half a page when you get there</em>).</p>
<p>Other than toys like <a href="clever-find-conditions-in-rails-without-sql-56.html">clever find conditions in Rails without SQL</a> and <a href="specify-rails-response-type-via-the-url-65.html">specifying response types in the URL</a>, you'll be able to look forward to some extreme REST goodies where Rails can route certain HTTP verbs directly to controller actions (e.g. a DELETE HTTP request would route to 'destroy', POST would route to 'create', etc) and the ability to pull data from other applications natively with ActiveResource <em>(I've hacked my own dirty version of this into apps I've developed, and this will be a life saver)</em>.</p>
