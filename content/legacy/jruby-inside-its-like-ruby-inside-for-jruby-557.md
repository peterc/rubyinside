<p>I'm proud to announce the tentative launch of.. <a href="http://www.jrubyinside.com/">JRuby Inside</a>, the latest addition to the Ruby Inside media empire (if two sites counts as an 'empire'!). <strong><a href="http://www.jrubyinside.com/">JRuby Inside</a></strong><strong> is like Ruby Inside, but for </strong><strong><a href="http://jruby.codehaus.org/">JRuby</a></strong><strong>, the Ruby implementation that runs on the Java Virtual Machine.</strong></p>
<p>Like Ruby Inside it has editorial direction. It's not just a bunch of links submitted by thousands of people, as you'd find on fine sites like DZone, Digg, Reddit, or del.icio.us. It features links from lots of different sources, but I choose which ones make it to the RSS feed (so you don't get overwhelmed with junk, repeat stories or spam), which to delete, and which I want to edit the titles of. That said, users <em>can</em> post their own stories or comments, but these will be monitored closely, and will only make it to the RSS feed if they're good.</p>
<p>Ruby Inside will continue to cover <em>major</em> or <em>cool</em> JRuby news, as occurs in the <a href="http://www.rubyinside.com/category/jruby/">JRuby category</a>, but JRuby Inside is the place to be to keep your pulse on top of happenings in the world of JRuby.</p>
