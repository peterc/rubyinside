<p><img src="/assets/2010/10/windows-ruby.jpg" width="63" height="58" alt="windows-ruby.jpg" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;">Back in August, <a href="microsoft-tires-of-ironruby-jimmy-schementi-jumps-ship-3639.html">Microsoft seemed to get tired of IronRuby</a> so its project leader Jimmy Schementi jumped ship while asking the Ruby community to step up and get involved in its future. Today, Microsoft has <a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/b/jasonz/archive/2010/10/21/new-components-and-contributors-for-ironpython-and-ironruby.aspx">announced new leadership for IronRuby</a> (and IronPython) and has effectively jettisoned it into the community as a true fully open source project.</p>
<p>So who's in charge of <a href="http://ironruby.net/">IronRuby</a> now? Jimmy Schementi, naturally, and Miguel de Icaza, the founder of the Mono and Gnome projects and generally all round super famous open source dude.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2010/10/icaza.jpg" width="196" height="205" alt="icaza.jpg" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<p>Schementi has <a href="http://blog.jimmy.schementi.com/2010/10/leadership-of-ironruby-and-ironpython.html">written about what the leadership changes and Microsoft's announcements mean</a> in the greater scheme of IronRuby's development. In short, Microsoft is no longer directly funding the projects but isn't restricting contributions or keeping code hidden behind the scenes anymore either (e.g. the IronRuby tools for Visual Studio).</p>
<p>Want to give IronRuby a try or get the source code? Head over to <a href="http://ironruby.net/">IronRuby's official homepage at ironruby.net</a>.</p>
