<p><img src="/assets/2009/02/passengerlogo.png" width="202" height="69" alt="passengerlogo.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;">Can you remember what a hideous chore it was to deploy Ruby-based apps (Rails apps being a key example) before early 2008? FastCGI, proxying schemes, plain old CGI - it was all a bit of a mess. It was so bad, in fact, that in January 2008 we posted <a href="no-true-mod_ruby-is-damaging-rubys-viability-on-the-web-693.html">No True "mod_ruby" Is Damaging Ruby's Viability On The Web</a> and kicked off a major discussion about it (115 comments!)</p>
<p>A couple of months later, an as-then unknown Dutch company called Phusion (headed by Ninh Bui and Hongli Lai) released <a href="http://www.modrails.com/">mod_rails</a> (a.k.a. Passenger), an Apache module that automatically handles server processes for Ruby apps and makes it a snap (just upload and touch a file) to deploy <em>any</em> Rack-based Ruby webapp with Apache - everyone has benefitted, including Sinatra, Merb and Ramaze development.</p>
<p>Phusion is <a href="http://blog.phusion.nl/2009/02/16/phusion-passenger-community-sponsorship-campaign/">now looking for help</a>. They're a company but they essentially <em>give away</em> their two main products: Passenger and <a href="http://www.rubyenterpriseedition.com/">Ruby Enterprise Edition</a>. You can pay for an "<a href="http://www.modrails.com/enterprise.html">enterprise license</a>" but it's really just a donation system. Now they're looking to specifically raise $14,000 to get the next version of Passenger out of the door. They're promising a lot - extended documentation, improved support and security, finalized Ruby 1.9 support, and, heck, too much to count.</p>
<p style="float: right; margin-left: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;"><a href="http://www.pledgie.com/campaigns/2975"><img alt="Click here to lend your support to: Phusion Passenger first community sponsorship and make a donation at www.pledgie.com !" src="http://www.pledgie.com/campaigns/2975.png?skin_name=chrome" border="0"></a></p>
<p>They have just $8,336 to go and if you want to help push forward the state of deploying Ruby-powered webapps, <a href="http://www.pledgie.com/campaigns/2975"><strong>click here to donate</strong></a> (or <a href="http://blog.phusion.nl/2009/02/16/phusion-passenger-community-sponsorship-campaign/">read more</a> about what they're achieving). All donors will be credited.</p>
<p>P.S. In May 2008, we ran <a href="28_mod_rails_and_passenger_resources-899.html">28 mod_rails / Passenger Resources to Help You Deploy Rails Applications Faster</a> that you might like to revisit.</p>
<p>P.P.S. Update: Ruddy 'eck - it's only 24 hours on and it's down to just $2854.70 to go.. nice one guys!</p>
