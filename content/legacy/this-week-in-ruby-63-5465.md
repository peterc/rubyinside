<p><img src="/assets/2011/10/paperboy-150x150.jpg" alt="" title="AA040001" width="150" height="150" class="alignnone size-thumbnail wp-image-5475" style="float: left; margin-right: 18px; border: 1px solid #666; margin-bottom: 18px">Back in 2008 and 2009, Ruby Inside had a long line of "Interesting Ruby Tidbits That Don’t Need Separate Posts" posts, aimed at sharing a collection of news and libraries in one hit. In the last year, I've shifted Ruby Inside to focusing on less frequent tutorials or investigative features and have been putting all of the news on <a href="http://rubyweekly.com/">Ruby Weekly</a>, my weekly newsletter.</p>
<p>There are still many, though, who would prefer to read the news in their RSS readers or on the Web, so I'm going to be taking the things I find for Ruby Weekly each week, doing a little reformatting, and sharing them here on Ruby Inside too. The longer articles will then slot in nicely in between :-)</p>
<p>So here we go, starting off from Ruby Weekly issue 63. You can check out <a href="http://rubyweekly.com/archive/">the full 63 issue archive</a>, if you like, or even <a href="http://rubyweekly.com/">sign up for Ruby Weekly here</a> if you weren't familiar with it. Or, of course, stay here on Ruby Inside and get the same (or more) each week - it's your choice!</p>
<p>Without further ado..</p>
<h3>Headlines</h3>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://pragprog.com/book/rr2/rails-recipes" style="font-weight: bold;">Rails Recipes: Rails 3 Edition (in Beta)</a><br>
Chad Fowler has been updating the Pragmatic Programmers' Rails Recipes book to Rails 3.1 standards. It's in beta now (as an e-book) with the full print release expected in December 2011.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2011/10/7/ann-rails-3-1-1" style="">Rails 3.1.1 Released: A Touch Up and Fixes Release</a></p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://blog.segment7.net/2011/10/08/rdoc-3-10" style="">RDoc 3.10 Released</a></p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://techcrunch.com/2011/10/10/spree-raises-1-5-million-from-true-ventures-aol-for-open-source-ecommerce-platform/" style="font-weight: bold;">Spree (Open Source Rails E-commerce System) Raises $1.5 Million</a><br>
Spree is a popular open source Rails e-commerce system and its creator has raised $1.5m in venture capital to take it even further (as well as offer Spree-based consulting). Tom Preston Werner (GitHub) and James Lindenbaum (Heroku) have joined as advisors.
</p>
</div>
<h3>Articles and Tutorials</h3>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://recipes.sinatrarb.com/" style="font-weight: bold;">Sinatra Recipes - Handy Recipes for Working with Sinatra</a><br>
Sinatra Recipes is a community contributed set of recipes and techniques for working with the popular Sinatra webapp library. There are examples of using many different libraries with Sinatra that are bound to prove useful, if only to learn from.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://www.jonathan-jackson.net/give-it-a-pry" style="font-weight: bold;">Give It A Pry</a><br>
Pry is a powerful REPL environment for Ruby and an ideal alternative to IRB. Jon Jackson shows off his top 5 reasons for switching to Pry - maybe they'll convince you too.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://dave.fayr.am/posts/2011-10-4-rubyists-already-use-monadic-patterns.html" style="font-weight: bold;">Rubyists Already Use Monadic Patterns</a><br>
Monadic programming is a pattern that comes up in a lot of code, just like most patterns. While associated with pure functional language like Haskell, Dave Fayram demonstrates how many patterns used by Ruby developers are monadic too.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://rubysource.com/creating-your-first-gem/" style="">Creating Your First Gem</a></p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://www.rubyinside.com/19walkthrough/" style="font-weight: bold;">The Ruby 1.9 Walkthrough: Go Deep on Ruby 1.9</a><br>
The Ruby 1.9 Walkthrough is a thorough, up-to-date video walkthrough of Ruby 1.9 - perfect for 1.8 developers who are unsure about the leap. Even Ruby 1.9 guru James Edward Gray II said he picked up plenty of stuff from it.. but beware, it's long :-)
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://shapeshed.com/journal/using-rbenv-to-manage-rubies/" style="">Using rbenv to Manage Rubies</a></p>
</div>
<h3>Presentations and Videos</h3>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://confreaks.net/videos/659-rubyconf2011-why-you-don-t-get-mock-objects" style="font-weight: bold;">Gregory Moeck: Why You Don't Get Mock Objects</a><br>
Not a big fan of mock objects in testing? Don't even know what mocks are? Get up to speed with Gregory Moeck who makes a convincing case for mocks in this RubyConf 2011 replay. In essence, if you're doing object orientation properly, you need mocks (ooh, controversial!)
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://www.slideshare.net/karmi/your-data-your-search-elasticsearch-euruko-2011" style="font-weight: bold;">Your Data, Your Search: ElasticSearch</a><br>
A wonderful Ruby-focused slide-deck by Karel Minarik (that was presented at EURUKO 2011) all about the concept of 'searching', the flaws in naive solutions, and a tour of the ElasticSearch system and using it from a Rails app.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://rubyconf11.merbist.com/#1" style="font-weight: bold;">Complex Ruby Concepts Simplified (Presentation)</a><br>
At RubyConf 2011, Matt Aimonetti gave a presentation that dug into Ruby's garbage collection, C interface, global interpreter lock, and more, and the slides are definitely easy (and worthwhile) to follow.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://net.tutsplus.com/tutorials/ruby/the-intro-to-rails-screencast-i-wish-i-had/" style="font-weight: bold;">The Intro to Rails Screencast I Wish I Had</a><br>
Over at NetTuts+, Jeffrey Way has put together an interesting 40 minute 'introduction to Rails' screencast that takes the high road and covers topics like TDD, auto testing, RSpec, and Capybara. It's an introduction for people who want to get it right first try.
</p>
</div>
<h3>Libraries and code</h3>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="https://github.com/karmi/tire" style="font-weight: bold;">Tire: A Rich Ruby API for the ElasticSearch Search Engine/Database System</a><br>
Tire is a Ruby client for ElasticSearch, written by Karel Minarik. ElasticSearch is a scalable, distributed, cloud-ready, highly-available, full-text search engine and database with powerfull aggregation features, communicating by JSON over RESTful HTTP, based on Lucene, written in Java.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="https://github.com/ryandotsmith/queue_classic" style="font-weight: bold;">Queue Classic: A Powerful PostgreSQL-Backed Queueing Library</a><br>
Queue Classic is a PostgreSQL-backed queueing library built by Ryan Smith of Heroku that focuses on concurrent job locking, minimizing database load and providing a simple, intuitive user experience.
</p>
</div>
