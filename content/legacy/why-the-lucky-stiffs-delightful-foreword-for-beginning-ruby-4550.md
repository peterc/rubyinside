<p><img src="/assets/2011/03/whys_matz.png" alt="" title="whys_matz" width="102" height="108" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4557" style="float: left; margin-right: 18px; margin-bottom: 12px; border: 1px solid #333">I started <em>Ruby Inside</em> in May 2006 as a promotional vehicle for my then in-progress book, <strong><a href="http://beginningruby.org/">Beginning Ruby.</a></strong>  It eventually went on to be published by <em>Apress</em> and is now on its second edition <a href="http://beginningruby.org/what-ive-earned-and-learned/">having sold quite a few copies.</a></p>
<p>It's typical to choose someone who's better known than you in the field to write a foreword for you to lend some legitimacy to your book and I only had one choice: <a href="http://www.smashingmagazine.com/2010/05/15/why-a-tale-of-a-post-modern-genius/">Why The Lucky Stiff.</a> As with many Rubyists, Why was a hero of mine and I wanted to go with the unusual route of an illustrated foreword. Surprisingly, Why readily accepted the challenge.</p>
<p>The foreword turned out great. Why disappeared for a few months after submitting his first drafts so we stuck with them but he had more planned. Many readers of <em>Beginning Ruby</em> have commented on how much they love his work, but today I discovered that several people I know well had never seen this work (because, of course, competent Rubyists don't need an introductory book). So here's a copy and paste of Why's foreword for Beginning Ruby for all to enjoy:</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2011/03/why1.png" alt="" title="why1" width="613" height="779" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4551"><img src="/assets/2011/03/why2.png" alt="" title="why2" width="621" height="774" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4552"><img src="/assets/2011/03/why31.png" alt="" title="why3" width="589" height="847" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4554"></p>
<p>If you want to check out Beginning Ruby, <a href="http://no.gd/begruby2.pdf">give this PDF a look.</a> The 2nd edition is a couple of years old now so ignore the Rails bits. Oh, and <em>shh...</em></p>
