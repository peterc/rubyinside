<p>Ola Bini (of JRuby fame) is <a href="http://olabini.com/blog/2008/09/paradigms-of-artificial-intelligence-programming-in-ruby/">starting</a> a series of blog posts about artificial intelligence programming in Ruby, based off of the landmark textbook "<a href="http://norvig.com/paip.html">Paradigms of Artificial Intelligence Programming: Case Studies in Common Lisp</a>" by Peter Norvig (currently the Director of Research at Google). PAIP is a significant work in the artificial intelligence world, and despite being published in 1992 it still has a lot of relevance.</p>
<p>Ola is working through the book and rewriting the examples to suit Ruby developers (with Norvig's permission) - this code will be available from <a href="http://github.com/olabini/paipr">a Github repository</a> as soon as it's written/updated. Ola says he'll be explaining all of the code as he goes along, so owning the book isn't a prerequisite.</p>
<p>Aside from the brief introduction to the concept, Ola has produced the first post in the series: <a href="http://olabini.com/blog/2008/09/language-generation/">Language Generation</a> - where he looks at the generation of natural language. If you have even a casual interest in artificial intelligence, this will be a good series to follow. You might also appreciate the following Ruby / AI resources:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<a href="http://web.media.mit.edu/~dustin/papers/ai_ruby_plugins/">AI Related Ruby Extensions</a> <a href="http://ai4r.rubyforge.org/"></a>
</li>
<li>
<a href="http://ai4r.rubyforge.org/">AI4R</a> - a library containing a number of AI functions for Rubyists</li>
<li>A <a href="http://ai-app-prog.rubyforge.org/">port of M. Tim Jones' AI "Application Programming" examples from C to Ruby</a> - a similar project to Ola's, but that appears to have fizzled out.</li>
</ul>
