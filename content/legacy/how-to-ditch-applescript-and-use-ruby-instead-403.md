<p>Matt Neuburg has put together a great article full of examples of <a href="http://www.macdevcenter.com/pub/a/mac/2007/02/27/replacing-applescript-with-ruby.html?CMP=OTC-13IV03560550&amp;ATT=Replacing+AppleScript+with+Ruby">using Ruby and AppScript in place of AppleScript</a> to script operations under OS X. I hadn't bothered to try these Ruby-&gt;OS X bridges yet, but I followed Matt's simple examples with amazement. It's so simple! Within two pages Matt moves on to using Ruby to instruct Microsoft Excel to produce a graph based on data provided from Ruby and it'll only take you up to ten minutes to read the whole thing and get that far. Excellent piece.</p>
<p>Matt seems to prefer to installing rb-appscript from source, but being a lazy-ass, I installed it in gem fashion with a simple <em>sudo gem install rb-appscript</em>. Your mileage may vary with this technique, but it worked for me.</p>
