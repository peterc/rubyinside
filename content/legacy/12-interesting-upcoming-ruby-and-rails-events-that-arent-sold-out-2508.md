<p>Wanted to go to <a href="http://jrubyconf.com/">JRubyConf</a> or <a href="http://www.rubyconf.org/">RubyConf</a> this year? Tough - they're sold out already. Luckily, though, there are some interesting upcoming events that you <i>can</i> still get in to - some online, some in multiple cities, some held in youth centers, some in hotels, and even one on a tropical island. Whew!</p>
<p><i>(Update: Removed Aloha on Rails as it has sold out - thanks to Tammer Saleh for the note.)</i></p>
<h3>Free "Optimizing Your Online Store" Webinar (Online; September 24, 2009)</h3>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/09/newrelic.png" width="144" height="30" alt="newrelic.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<p>New Relic is running <a href="http://www.newrelic.com/webinar">a free webinar event</a> on Thursday, September 24 for developers and customers interested in optimizing e-commerce sites ready for the 2009 holiday season. The webinar will feature New Relic customer <i>SheetMusicPlus.com</i> and will provide tips for getting your online store prepared.</p>
<p>Naturally, being a New Relic event, the focus will be on New Relic's RPM product and a special webinar-only discount will be offered. Given the quality of New Relic's other materials though, I doubt it's going to be a sales pitch from start to finish. <a href="http://www.newrelic.com/webinar">Click here to register.</a> <i>(I've just been told the first 20 people to register are entered in a draw to win an iPod nano.)</i></p>
<h3>RubyFoo (London, UK; October 2-3, 2009)</h3>
<p><b><img src="/assets/2009/09/rubyfoo.png" width="139" height="37" alt="rubyfoo.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></b></p>
<p><b><a href="http://jaoo.dk/ruby-london-2009/">RubyFoo</a></b> is a Ruby conference taking place in London in October, 2009. It's being put on by Trifork, the company behind the successful JAOO developer conferences. RubyFoo is a sort of follow up to the RubyFools conference they put on in Copenhagen, Denmark last year. Speakers include Sam Aaron, Ola Bini (JRuby), Adam Wiggins, Aslak Hellesoy, and Matz, and the general theme of the conference is "communicative programming." <b>Note: You can use the promotion code of "<i>Rubyinside</i>" to get £50 off registration.</b></p>
<h3>Rails Roadshow (USA; October and November, 2009)</h3>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/09/railsroadshow.png" width="114" height="76" alt="railsroadshow.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;">The <a href="http://www.railsroadshow.com/"><b>Rails Roadshow</b></a> is a series of five free events being organized by esteemed Rails hosting company <i>Engine Yard</i>. In order:</p>
<ul>
<li>October 26 - <i>Boston, MA</i>
</li>
<li>October 27 - <i>Chicago, IL</i>
</li>
<li>October 29 - <i>Austin, TX</i>
</li>
<li>October 30 - <i>Los Angeles, CA</i>
</li>
<li>November 2 - <i>Seattle, WA</i>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Despite being an Engine Yard-heavy event, the schedule is quite varied with speakers from Amazon, New Relic, and CVSDude in addition to those from Engine Yard. The theme is "performance in the cloud." For free, it seems like a nice morning out (the event finishes at midday) and you get breakfast - what's not to like?</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/rsla.png" width="72" height="66" alt="rsla.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>Rails Summit Latin America 2009 (São Paulo, Brazil; October 13-14, 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://railssummit.locaweb.com.br/en/pages/home"><b>Rails Summit Latin America</b></a> comes back for a second run in 2009 - taking place between October 13-14 in sunny São Paulo, Brazil. Last year's event attracted 550 attendees, so for a regional Ruby/Rails conference it did amazingly. This year it costs 400 Brazilian Real to attend (about $200).</p>
<p>Speakers lined up to speak so far include Bryan Liles, Carlos Brando, Chad Fowler, Gregg Pollack, Ilya Grigorik, Jason Seifer, Matt Aimonetti, Obie Fernandez, Pratik Naik, Rich Kilmer, and several prominent South American Rubyists. It's a bit of an all-star affair!</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/railscamp.png" width="85" height="62" alt="railscamp.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>Rails Camps (Margate, UK and Melbourne, Australia; October / November 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://railscamps.com/"><b>Rails Camps</b></a> is a series of Rails related get-togethers, run in a lo-fi style in, usually, Australia and the United Kingdom. Two Rails Camps are on the calendar for late 2009. The <a href="http://railscamps.com/#uk_october_2009">first</a> in Margate, UK (an interesting seaside resort, to say the least) on October 16-19 and the <a href="http://railscamps.com/#au_november_2009">second</a> in Melbourne, Australia on November 20-23. Previous reports show that these events seem to go down really well, especially if you want to get away from it all for a few days and hang out with fellow geeks - so check it out.</p>
<h3>RubyEnRails (Amsterdam, Netherlands; October 30-31, 2009)</h3>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/09/rer2009.png" width="128" height="62" alt="rer2009.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<p>Where Euruko is Europe's equivalent of RubyConf, <a href="http://2009.rubyenrails.nl/?locale=en">RubyEnRails</a> is starting to look like Europe's answer to RailsConf. This year, RubyEnRails takes place in Amsterdam on October 30 and 31 and speakers include Jeremy Kemper, Yehuda Katz, Jonathan Weiss, Eloy Duran, Bart ten Brinke, Willem van Bergen, and Edwin Vlieg. It only costs 79 euro (about $120 or £70) and it sounds like it's been a fun event in previous years.</p>
<p><i>Note: I always seem to miss some awesome events in these roundups so, as always, please leave comments recommending your own events, those I've forgotten, etc.</i></p>
