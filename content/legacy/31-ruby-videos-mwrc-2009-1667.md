<p><img src="/assets/2009/03/james-britt-ruby-superstar.gif" width="110" height="110" alt="james-britt-ruby-superstar.gif" style="margin-right:12px; margin-bottom: 12px; float: left; border:1px #000000 solid;"> The always popular <a href="http://mtnwestrubyconf.org/2009/">MountainWest RubyConf</a> took place again this year on March 13-14 and the always awesome Confreaks team was on hand to record all of the presentations. <em>Already</em> they have <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/">31 videos of MWRC 2009</a> up and ready to view in both HD (720p) and 640x360 MPEG4 formats. This is a <em>goldmine</em> of viewing and even if you don't get to a single Ruby conference this year, these videos could do 90% of the work for you.</p>
<p>So what rocked? Well, I haven't had time to watch all 31 videos, but from what I've heard and from those I have seen, here are some recommendations:</p>
<div style="margin-left: 1.5em">
<p><strong>James Britt:</strong> <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/14-mar-2009-17-00-wii-ruby-all-work-and-no-play-just-wont-do-james-britt.html"><strong>Wii Ruby: All work and no play just won't do.</strong></a> <strong>-</strong> Until this video I'd never seen James Britt (pictured - of Ruby-Doc fame) in action, but he's a really good presenter - tells it straight, doesn't waste time. He talks about using Ruby (JRuby, specifically) to pick up data from the Wiimote's buttons and sensors. Pretty novel, and fun, stuff.</p>
<p><strong>Yehuda Katz:</strong> <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/13-mar-2009-11-55-the-great-rails-refactor-yehuda-katz.html"><strong>The Great Rails Refactor</strong></a> <strong>-</strong> Yehuda, prominent Merb developer, presents a walkthrough of the Merb/Rails merge and how things will progress from here. If Rails 3.0 is interesting to you, watch this. His look at ORM agnosticism with ActionORM is particularly cool.</p>
<p><strong>Jeremy Evans:</strong> <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/13-mar-2009-12-30-sequel-jeremy-evans.html"><strong>Sequel</strong></a> <strong>-</strong> Jeremy Evans talks about <a href="http://sequel.rubyforge.org/">Sequel</a>, the lightweight database toolkit (Jeremy is now the maintainer but didn't create the project). Jeremy presents a whirlwind walkthrough with very text and code heavy slides. This presentation style tends to be a bit heavy in real life, but with a video it's great because you can pause if you want to read the slides :)</p>
<p><strong>Jeremy Hinegardner:</strong> <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/13-mar-2009-16-10-ffi-jeremy-hinegardner.html"><strong>FFI</strong></a> <strong>-</strong> Jeremy looks at Ruby's <a href="ruby-ffi-library-calling-external-libraries-now-easier-1293.html">new(ish) FFI (Foreign Function Interface) support</a>, specifically in the context of making cross-language extensions.</p>
<p><strong>Gregory Brown:</strong> <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/13-mar-2009-19-00-gibberishsimple-gregory-brown.html"><strong>Gibberish::Simple</strong></a> <strong>-</strong> <a href="prawn-ruby-pdf-library-987.html">Prawn</a> creator Gregory Brown presents a five minute lightning talk that looks at how to localize Ruby 1.9 apps.</p>
<p><strong>Brian Ford:</strong> <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/13-mar-2009-19-30-rubinius-brian-ford.html"><strong>Rubinius (1.0)</strong></a> - Engine Yard developer Brian Ford presents a five minute lightning talk on the Rubinius Ruby implementation. Not bad work considering he prepared the presentation at the last minute! If you want a very quick wrap up of what Rubinius is about, check it out.</p>
</div>
<p>And.. of course, <a href="http://mwrc2009.confreaks.com/">there are 25 others!</a> They cover stuff like writing adapters for DataMapper, BDD, usability, VoIP, DSLs, machine learning, GUI testing, workflows, and text to speech. Got a favorite and think we should all watch it? Leave a comment here!</p>
<p>As an aside, I'm looking forward to the <a href="http://scotlandonrails.com/">Scotland on Rails</a> (currently taking place in Edinburgh) videos coming along in the next few weeks (hopefully) but MountainWest has left them extremely big shoes to fill.</p>
