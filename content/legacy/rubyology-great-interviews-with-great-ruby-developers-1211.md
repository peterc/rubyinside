<p><img src="/assets/2008/09/rubyology.jpg" width="154" height="59" alt="rubyology.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"><a href="http://www.rubyology.com/">Rubyology</a> is a Ruby-based podcast chaired by Chris Matthieu. Its focus is on interviewing interesting Ruby (and Rails) developers - finding out about their projects, what makes them tick, and extracting their insights into the Ruby and Rails worlds.</p>
<p>For some reason, Rubyology hasn't had a lot of love in the Ruby blogosphere, and after listening to several great interviews over the past few months, I've decided this is a great time to recommend you check it out if you're not already a subscriber.</p>
<p>Recent highlights include:</p>
<ul>
<li>A <a href="http://www.rubyology.com/podcasts/show/66">two</a>-<a href="http://www.rubyology.com/podcasts/show/67">part</a> interview with Powerset developers Dave Fayram and Tom Preston Werner</li>
<li>An <a href="http://www.rubyology.com/podcasts/show/65">interview with the Rails Envy guys</a> (it's as entertaining as you'd expect)</li>
<li>An <a href="http://www.rubyology.com/podcasts/show/64">interview with Ezra Zygmuntowicz</a> (developer on the Merb and Rubinius projects)</li>
<li>An <a href="http://www.rubyology.com/podcasts/show/69">interview with Jay Phillips</a> (developer of Adhearsion)</li>
</ul>
<p>Of course, don't forget about Geoffrey Grosenbach's <a href="http://podcast.rubyonrails.org/">Rails Podcast</a> either - it's not all Rails focused these days. The latest episode is an <a href="http://podcast.rubyonrails.org/system/audio/2008/rails-85-jim-weirich.mp3">interview with Jim Weirich</a>.</p>
