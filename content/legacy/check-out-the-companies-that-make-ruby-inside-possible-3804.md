<h3>Linode — Xen VPS Hosting</h3>
<p><a href="http://www.linode.com/">Linode</a> is a Xen-based VPS (virtual private server) hosting service that's now in its 8th year. Plans start at $19.95 per month for a plan with 512MB of RAM, 16GB of storage, and 200GB of transfer bandwidth. Want 1GB of RAM, 32GB of storage, and 400GB of bandwidth? That's $39.95 per month. Linode's major advantages over the competition are reliability and performance (as shown in <a href="http://journal.uggedal.com/vps-performance-comparison">these performance tests</a> by Eivind Uggedal) and I've even "downgraded" from dedicated servers to using Linode because they're almost as good but for a fraction of the cost!</p>
<p><em>A non-disclaimer: Ruby Inside is hosted with Linode but the hosting is all paid for separately and is not related to their sponsorship. I'll be sticking with them - they provide an amazing service.</em></p>

<h3>New Relic — On-demand Application Management</h3>
<p><a href="http://www.newrelic.com/RPMlite-rails.html?utm_source=rubyinside&amp;utm_medium=banner&amp;utm_content=125x125&amp;utm_campaign=rpm">New Relic</a> is a Java and Ruby application performance and reliability monitoring and management service that started life as a Rails-only service (and it's still great for that!). It's truly enterprise-grade software but with the flexibility and accessibility of annual, monthly, or "on demand" pricing, catering to nearly all types of customer. With New Relic you can monitor your apps, find slow transactions, see specific SQL queries, and even run a code-level thread profile. <em>Trivia: New Relic is an anagram of founder "Lew Cirne"'s name!</em></p>
<p><a href="http://scoutapp.com/r/ri"><img src="sponsors/scout.png" style="float: right; margin-left: 18px; margin-bottom: 12px"></a></p>
<h3>Scout — Hosted Server Monitoring</h3>
<p><a href="http://scoutapp.com/r/ri">Scout</a> is a simple yet highly flexible hosted server and application monitoring service. It's powered by plugins written in Ruby that are designed to cover all sorts of use cases (monitoring CPU, networking, IO status, MongoDB instances, etc.) and you can write your own if you have other requirements. Getting started with Scout takes just two commands (installing a gem and running the "scout" process). In most cases, it takes less than five minutes to log on to your server, install the Scout agent, and start seeing data in your control panel.</p>
