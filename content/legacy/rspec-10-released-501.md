<p><img src="/assets/2007/05/rspec-1.jpg" height="207" width="348" border="1" hspace="4" vspace="4" alt="Rspec-1"></p>
<p>The RSpec development team have just <a href="http://groups.google.com/group/ruby-talk-google/browse_thread/thread/d30f3cf94b268488/481685879bd9c05f">announced</a> the release of <a href="http://rspec.rubyforge.org/">RSpec 1.0</a>, the first major version of what has become a popular "<a href="http://behaviour-driven.org/">Behavior Driven Development</a>" library for Ruby.</p>
<p>If you're fresh to RSpec, check out <a href="http://rspec.rubyforge.org/">the official home page</a> for a compelling example, or straight from the mouths of the RSpec team:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>RSpec provides a Domain Specific Language for describing and verifying the behaviour of Ruby code with executable examples.</p>
<p>Some people like to call these examples "tests". In fact, they are. But we believe that tests have equally important value as documentation and as a design aid, and that the testing nomenclature and syntax in most unit testing frameworks keep too much focus on only the testing value, and hide the design and documentation value.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>40 contributors have been involved with RSpec, along with 5 core developers, making RSpec one of the most popular collective Ruby projects.</p>
