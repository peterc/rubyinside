<p>Here's a list of some prominent forthcoming Ruby and Rails events scheduled through to the end of the year. Only events with tickets ready to buy right now are included - events which have already sold out are <i>not</i> included.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/lsrc.png" width="85" height="88" alt="lsrc.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>Lone Star Ruby Conference 2009 (Austin, Texas; August 27-29, 2009)</h3>
<p>The <strong>third</strong> annual <a href="http://lonestarrubyconf.com/"><b>Lone Star Ruby Conference (LSRC)</b></a> is heating up and right around the corner, running for three days, August 27-29 in Austin, <span class="caps">TX. LSRC</span> 2009 offers a full day of advanced training on Thursday in addition to the two days of Ruby-packed, dual-track speaking sessions on Friday and Saturday. And, back again for an encore performance, is the author of Ruby, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yukihiro_Matsumoto" title="a.k.a. Matz">Yukihiro Matsumoto</a></p>
<p>Registration is $350 for the conference (meals includes) and an additional $300 for the advanced training. A <a href="http://lonestarrubyconf.com/program.">full outline of available training sessions and presentations</a> is available.</p>
<h3>RubyRX (Washington DC; September 10-11, 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://www.nfjsone.com/conference/washington_dc/2009/09/home"><b>RubyRX</b></a> is a professional Ruby conference covering what you'd expect at a professional Ruby conference, namely: Ruby development, Rails, JRuby, Ruby tools, frameworks, testing, best practices, and so forth. You get a two-fer with this conference as the AgileRX conference is taking place at the same time at the same place!</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/windy-city-rails.png" width="137" height="35" alt="windy_city_rails.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>WindyCityRails (Chicago, Illinois; September 12)</h3>
<p><a href="http://windycityrails.org/"><b>WindyCityRails</b></a> is a one-day conference taking place in Chicago (home of better pizza than New York, 37signals and, of course, Rails creator, David Heinemeier Hansson) in September. You get seven sessions and two tutorials for your money with Ryan Singer (37signals), Noel Rappin, Ben Scofield, John McCaffrey, Dean Wampler, Yehuda Katz, and David Elsinger to keep you entertained.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/rubyfoo.png" width="137" height="36" alt="rubyfoo.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>RubyFoo (London; October 2-3, 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://jaoo.dk/ruby-london-2009"><b>RubyFoo</b></a> is a Ruby conference taking place in London in October, 2009. It's being put on by Trifork, the company behind the successful JAOO developer conferences. RubyFoo is a sort of follow up to the <a href="http://jaoo.dk/ruby-cph-2008/">RubyFools</a> conference they put on in Copenhagen, Denmark last year. Speakers lined up so far include Sam Aaron, Ola Bini (JRuby), Aslak Hellesoy, and Matz.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/aloha.png" width="107" height="62" alt="aloha.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>Aloha On Rails (Hawaii; October 4-6, 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://www.alohaonrails.com/"><b>Aloha on Rails</b></a> is a brave new entry to the Rails conference circuit, taking place in Hawaii in early October. If you've always wanted to try and get a Hawaii vacation on the company dime, this is the time to build up your powers of persuasion. The conference bills itself as "the premier destination event for Ruby on Rails and Web development" and promises three unique days (2 for conference, 1 for tutorials) of Ruby and Rails related fun.<br>
The speaker list for Aloha is pretty significant. Names include Chad Fowler, Obie Fernandez, Gregg Pollack, Desi McAdam, Charles Nutter, Yehuda Katz, Sarah Mei, Tammer Saleh, Chad Pytel, Blake Mizerany, Pat Maddox, and Ezra Zygmuntowicz.. and that's only the start of it - <a href="http://www.alohaonrails.com/speakers/">see all of them</a>.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/rsla.png" width="72" height="66" alt="rsla.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>Rails Summit Latin America 2009 (São Paulo, Brazil; October 13-14, 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://railssummit.locaweb.com.br/en/pages/home"><b>Rails Summit Latin America</b></a> comes back for a second run in 2009 - taking place between October 13-14 in sunny São Paulo, Brazil. Last year's event attracted 550 attendees, so for a regional Ruby/Rails conference it did amazingly. This year it costs 400 Brazilian Real to attend (about $200).</p>
<p>Speakers lined up to speak so far include Bryan Liles, Carlos Brando, Chad Fowler, Gregg Pollack, Ilya Grigorik, Jason Seifer, Matt Aimonetti, Obie Fernandez, Pratik Naik, Rich Kilmer, and several prominent South American Rubyists. It's a bit of an all-star affair!</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/railscamp.png" width="85" height="62" alt="railscamp.png" style="float:right; margin-bottom:12px; margin-left:12px;"></p>
<h3>Rails Camps (Margate, UK and Melbourne, Australia; October / November 2009)</h3>
<p><a href="http://railscamps.com/"><b>Rails Camps</b></a> is a series of Rails related get-togethers, run in a lo-fi style in, usually, Australia and the United Kingdom. Two Rails Camps are on the calendar for late 2009. The <a href="http://railscamps.com/#uk_october_2009">first</a> in Margate, UK (an interesting seaside resort, to say the least) on October 16-19 and the <a href="http://railscamps.com/#au_november_2009">second</a> in Melbourne, Australia on November 20-23. Previous reports show that these events seem to go down really well, especially if you want to get away from it all for a few days and hang out with fellow geeks - so check it out.</p>
