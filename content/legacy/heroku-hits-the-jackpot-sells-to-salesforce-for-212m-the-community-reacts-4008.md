<p><img src="/assets/2010/12/anteater.png" width="87" height="85" alt="anteater.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px; border:1px #000000 solid;">TechCrunch <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2010/12/08/breaking-salesforce-buys-heroku-for-212-million-in-cash/">reports</a> that Salesforce.com is to acquire the popular <a href="http://heroku.com/">Heroku</a> Ruby app hosting service for approximately <b>$212 million in cash</b>. We first <a href="heroku-an-online-rails-development-and-app-hosting-environment-647.html">posted about Heroku back in November 2007</a> (18 months before it even <a href="heroku-the-git-powered-ruby-hosting-platform-goes-commercial-1704.html">went commercial</a>) and since then they've gone from strength to strength. Congratulations to the team!</p>
<p>Despite being under embargo with Salesforce on the <a href="salesforce-com-launches-database-com-the-first-enterprise-level-cloud-database-3974.html">recent Database.com story</a> and being alerted to almost every other Heroku story in the last few years, TechCrunch obviously got an exclusive so <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2010/12/08/breaking-salesforce-buys-heroku-for-212-million-in-cash/">read that for the details</a>, <a href="http://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=1982489">read this Hacker News thread</a> for some smart discussion, and read <a href="http://blog.heroku.com/archives/2010/12/8/the_next_level/">Heroku's own impressions</a> on what the sale means for them and their customers (in short, Heroku will remain quite independent within the Salesforce.com ecosystem and the brand will live on).</p>
<p>Whatever you make of the sale - and reactions on Twitter are mixed - $212 million is a big deal in the Ruby world and it seriously validates the Ruby webapp hosting industry. A few months ago there were rumors of Engine Yard being in acquisition talks too and perhaps a few more companies will go under the hammer soon. <i>(If your flush-with-cash company wants to either acquire or exclusively sponsor Ruby Inside, Rails Inside, RubyFlow and Ruby Weekly, get in touch! ;-))</i></p>
