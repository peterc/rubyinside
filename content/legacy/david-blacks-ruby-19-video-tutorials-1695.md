<p><img src="/assets/2009/04/dblackenvycasts.jpg" width="400" height="197" alt="dblackenvycasts.jpg"></p>
<p>Popular Ruby trainer and author <a href="http://dablog.rubypal.com/">David A Black</a> has partnered up with <a href="http://envycasts.com/">Envycasts</a> (from the <a href="http://www.railsenvy.com/">Rails Envy</a> guys) to produce <a href="http://envycasts.com/products/ruby-19-package-deal">a set of two Ruby 1.9 video tutorials / screencasts</a>. You can buy each part (part <a href="http://envycasts.com/products/ruby-19-part-1">1</a>, <a href="http://envycasts.com/products/ruby-19-part-2">2</a>) on its own for $9 or the two together as <a href="http://envycasts.com/products/ruby-19-package-deal">a set</a> for $16. If you choose the set, you'll also get a 40%-off coupon for David's new book, <a href="http://manning.com/black2/">The Well Grounded Rubyist</a> (the followup to <em>Ruby for Rails</em>).</p>
<p>I've watched both of the videos and they're up to the usual extremely high production standards that Envycasts is becoming known for (see <a href="http://www.railsinside.com/screencasts/137-envy-casts-releases-rails-22-screencast-and-pdf.html">this review of their Rails 2.2 screencast and PDF</a>). Both videos are about 40 minutes long and the first covers hashes, arrays, symbols, enumerators, the Enumerable module, and RubyGems, while the second looks at some less obvious enhancements including new block variable features, String class updates, encodings, and a plethora of new methods that are newly available to all Ruby objects (as well as the new BasicObject class).</p>
<p>David has dug up some interesting examples and intriguing methods that aren't being used much by Rubyists yet, due to their newness, so even if you've got the basic hang of Ruby 1.9, the second video is sure to throw up some things you've hadn't seen before, such as <code>instance_exec</code>, <code>tap</code>, <code>public_send</code>, and <code>ancestors</code>.</p>
<h3>5 Copies To Be Won! Read on...</h3>

The <strong>first five unique people</strong> who post a comment on this post using a valid e-mail address (in the field, to keep it hidden from the general public, if you wish) and a suggestion of a screencast that you'd like to see produced in future (perhaps even with a specific suggestion of who should host it!) will receive a promo code to use. So.. get going with those ideas.. shoot from the hip! Gregg Pollack will leave a comment to follow up.</p>
<p>Lastly, don't forget to check out <a href="http://envycasts.com/">Envycasts' range of videos</a>. The <a href="http://envycasts.com/products/scaling-ruby">Scaling Ruby</a> one is particularly cool.</p>
