<p>More jobs - more opportunities! It's not entirely Rails-only opportunities this time around, although all the positions are in the US (primarily in San Francisco). The <a title="Ruby Inside Job Board" href="ruby-inside-job-board-446.html">Ruby Inside Job Board</a> (costs $99 for a 60 day listing - and you get featured on Ruby Inside like this) is the source for most of the positions.</p>
<p>So, on with this month's interesting opportunities:</p>

They're looking for a Ruby developer with computer science fundamentals to join their team. Knowledge of Rails, Merb, Capistrano, Git, Linux, AWS, and Ajax would all be very useful.</p>

Causecast is a "one stop shop for all things cause, giving nonprofits, leaders, and members opportunities to collaborate, share their experiences, and promote the causes they care about."</p>

The company is looking for a Rails engineer to help develop the front-end features that will make Justin.tv even more interesting and fun to use in the future.</p>

Ruby and Rails experience is a big plus but not an absolute necessity. Instead a belief in the way of "Agile" including story carding, pair programming and TDD is necessary, as well as a relocation to sunny Jacksonville Beach, Florida.</p>
<p><img src="/assets/2008/10/visualcv.png" width="114" height="41" alt="visualcv.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"><a href="http://ruby.jobamatic.com/a/jbb/job-details/33487"><strong>Ruby on Rails Web Developers</strong></a> <strong>(Reston, VA):</strong> VisualCV is a Web 2.0 startup in Northern Virginia that provides customized résumé services - indeed, they're "reinventing the resume." They're <a href="http://ruby.jobamatic.com/a/jbb/job-details/33488">also looking for Quality Assurance Engineers</a>.</p>

While they want to hire 1-2 employees, a qualified intern or freelancer may be considered.</p>
