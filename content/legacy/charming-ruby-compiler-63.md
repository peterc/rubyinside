<p>The <a href="http://www.dtek.chalmers.se/~d00farre/thesis/">Charming Ruby Compiler</a> is charming not just by name, but by nature. It's a thesis project at the Computer Science Department at <a href="http://www.chalmers.se/">Chalmers University of Technology</a> in Sweden to develop <strong>a proof of concept Ruby compiler that compiles to the C-- intermediate language</strong>. So far, they've only got the basics working, but it's an interesting project, if only to learn about <a href="http://www.cminusminus.org/">C--</a>, an interesting 'portable assembly language' that makes life easier for compiler developers. It seems a little like <a href="http://www.parrotcode.org/">Parrot</a>, and is funded by Microsoft Research and the National Science Foundation.</p>
