<p>This month I'm the guest judge and challenge provider of <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/01/blogging-contest-february-challenge.html">On Ruby and Apress's "Ruby Blogging Contest"</a>. The challenge question is "How has Ruby blown or stretched your mind?" If you blog your answer to that, you could win three free books from Apress. There's a bit more to it than that though, so check out <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/01/blogging-contest-february-challenge.html">the challenge in full</a> and get thinking.<br>
All you have to do is blog about how Ruby has blown or stretched your minds (the more citation of actual language features or constructs the better) and then post a link to your blog post in the comments section of <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/01/blogging-contest-february-challenge.html">this page</a>. If you win the contest, you get the choice of three books from technical publishers, Apress. <a href="http://www.apress.com/">Apress</a> have hundreds of books covering the gamut of programming knowledge on offer, and are also the publishers of my forthcoming book, <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ruby-Professional-Peter-Cooper/dp/1590597664">"Beginning Ruby: From Novice to Professional."</a> </p>
<p>I'm really looking forward to seeing what people have been really surprised and excited by in Ruby, especially if I can learn a few new tricks myself! If you're more of a reader than a writer though, or if you don't even have a blog, perhaps the entries to last month's contest, "<a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/01/win-books-by-blogging.html">How has Ruby on Rails made you a better programmer?</a>" will make interesting reading for you.</p>
