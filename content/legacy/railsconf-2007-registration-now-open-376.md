<p>Here's the official announcement:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>On Your Mark...Get Set...GO! Registration for RailsConf 2007 Is Now Open!</p>
<p>There are two good reasons to register now for RailsConf 2007. One, last year's inaugural conference sold out in just a few days. Two, you'll save $150 if you register before March 19. So don't wait -- <a href="http://conferences.oreillynet.com/pub/w/51/register.html">register today</a>.</p>
<p>Co-produced by O'Reilly Media and Ruby Central, RailsConf is the largest official conference dedicated to everything Rails. This year, it's taking place May 17-20 at the Oregon Convention Center in Portland, and it promises to be the best conference yet.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>DHH has <a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2007/2/2/railsconf-07-opens-for-registration">a little more to say</a> about it, but don't hang around.. <a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2007/2/2/one-third-of-railsconf-07-seats-gone">one third of the seats are sold already</a> and the announcement only went out a few hours ago! Have a good time in Portland folks. Alongside this, Benjamin Curtis has created <a href="http://railsconf2007.conferencemeetup.com/">a 'conference meetup' site for RailsConf 2007</a> where you can see who's attending and presenting and get in touch with people you're interested in meeting at the event.</p>
