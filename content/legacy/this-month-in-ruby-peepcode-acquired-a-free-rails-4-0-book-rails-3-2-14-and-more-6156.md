<p>Welcome to a roundup of Ruby news, articles, videos, and more, for July 2013 cobbled together from my e-mail newsletter, <a href="http://rubyweekly.com/">Ruby Weekly.</a> </p>
<p>Highlights include: PeepCode acquired by Pluralsight, Practicing Ruby archives made public, Rails 3.2.14, and an interesting interview with Matz.</p>
<h3>Featured</h3>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://www.rubyflow.com/items/9606-practicing-ruby-s-public-archives" style="font-weight: bold;">The First Four Volumes of Practicing Ruby, Now Available Online</a><br>
Practicing Ruby is a high quality, paid Ruby journal run by Gregory Brown, but he's made archives of over 60 articles available to the public. There's a ton of stuff to enjoy here.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="https://peepcode.com/blog/2013/leveling-up" style="font-weight: bold;">PeepCode Acquired by Pluralsight</a><br>
Ruby and Rails screencasting pioneer Geoffrey Grosenbach has announced he has sold Peepcode to Pluralsight, a large online training provider.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://myronmars.to/n/dev-blog/2013/07/the-plan-for-rspec-3" style="font-weight: bold;">The Plan for RSpec 3</a><br>
RSpec 2.0 was released in October 2010 and RSpec 2.14 will be the last RSpec 2 feature release. Work on RSpec 3 has begun and Myron Marston shares what's going to be involved.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2013/7/16/Rails-3-2-14-rc2-has-been-released/" style="font-weight: bold;">Rails 3.2.14 RC1 and RC2 Released</a><br>
A variety of bug fixes for Rails 3.2 <a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2013/7/12/Rails-3-2-14-rc1-has-been-released/">arrived in 3.2.14 RC1</a> with one minor regression fixed in RC2. Final due soon.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2013/7/23/Rails-3-2-14-has-been-released/" style="">Rails 3.2.14 Released</a></p>

<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
  <a href="http://fredwu.me/post/54175219257/the-future-of-computing-the-future-of-computer"><img src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/nlga/uploads/item/image/12267/125.png" width="133" height="100" style="float: right; margin-left: 14px; margin-bottom: 16px; border: 1px solid #1173c7"></a>
<p><a href="http://fredwu.me/post/54175219257/the-future-of-computing-the-future-of-computer" style="font-weight: bold;">The Future of Computing - An Interview with Matz</a><br>
Last year, Ruby's creator Yukihiro 'Matz' Matsumoto released a book called <a href="http://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/4822234630/">The Future of Computing</a> (only in Japanese, I believe) and did an interview with a Chinese publisher. Fred Wu has translated it into English.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="http://myronmars.to/n/dev-blog/2013/07/rspec-2-14-is-released" style="font-weight: bold;">RSpec 2.14 Released</a><br>
Myron Marston unveils the last 2.x feature release of the popular spec framework and announces work is well underway for the future RSpec 3. 2.14 includes a new feature called 'spies' which is shown off here.
</p>
</div>
<div style="margin: 16px 0px;" class="item">
<p><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5ZjwEPupybw" style="font-weight: bold;">Functional Programming and Ruby</a><br>
At GoRuCo 2013, Pat Shaughnessy gave a 40 minute talk comparing Haskell (a functional language) to Ruby and looked at how to implement common functional patterns in Ruby. Well explained and backed by good slides.
</p>
