<p><img src="/assets/2009/08/why-so-serious.png" width="84" height="86" alt="why-so-serious.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></p>
<p>Long-time Rubyist and the community's own resident crazy genius, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Why_the_lucky_stiff">whytheluckystiff</a> (a.k.a. _why) <a href="http://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=773106">seems to have gone missing</a>. Not only has he deleted his Twitter account (<a href="http://twitter.com/_why">@_why</a>) but <a href="http://github.com/why">his Github repositories</a> and all of his great Ruby related Web sites - <i>poignantguide.net, hackety.org, whytheluckystiff.net</i>, and <i>shoooes.net</i> are all down and not even resolving at DNS level.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.scribd.com/doc/2236084/Whys-Poignant-Guide-to-Ruby">The Poignant Guide</a>, <a href="fast-html-parsing-in-ruby-with-hpricot-125.html">Hpricot</a>, Markaby, <a href="redcloth-4-released-962.html">RedCloth</a>, <a href="whys-shoes-grows-up-1014.html">Shoes</a>, Camping, and Try Ruby (a Web version of <i>irb</i>) are considered important by Rubyists not only for their usefulness but for their significant contribution to Ruby's culture, and if _why has truly fallen off the grid, it's a big deal. That said, this appears to be either a deliberate attempt to disappear or a major hack and not just a bunch of coincidental outages. In either case, hopefully he'll be back soon.</p>
<p>What's the motive? We have nothing definite, but Hacker News user <i>fizx</i> quoted a recent tweet from <i>why</i> before he deleted his account:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>programming is rather thankless. u see your works become replaced by superior ones in a year. unable to run at all in a few more.</p>
</blockquote>
