<p><a href="http://railscasts.com/">Railscasts</a>, maintained by Ryan Bates, continues to release one great free screencast after another, with 9 so far this month, although we're going to include one from June 29 for good measure. We <a href="railscasts-free-ruby-on-rails-screencasts-426.html">first looked at Railscasts</a> a few months ago, so if you haven't visited again since, it's worth it. Here are the latest screencasts:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/60">Testing Without Fixtures</a> - Fed up with using fixtures for your tests? Find a better way.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/51">will_paginate</a> - A look at the "new way" to do pagination in Rails using the will_paginate plugin.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/52">Updating Through Checkboxes</a> - How to perform actions on multiple items selected with checkboxes in a Rails view.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/53">Handling Exceptions</a> - Learn about exceptions and how to handle them in Rails apps.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/56">The Logger</a> - No, it's not about felling trees. Learn how to send messages to the log and how to customize it.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/58">How to Make a Generator</a> - A look at building your own generator, much like the ones that come with Rails.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://railscasts.com/episodes/59">Optimistic Locking</a> - How to resolve situations when two people want to update the same record at the same time.</li>
</ul>
<p>At the same time, Geoffrey Grosenbach, curator of PeepCode (and a new sponsor of Ruby Inside), has been storming ahead with his own set of screencasts, a few of which will be of direct interest to Rubyists / Rails developers:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<a href="http://peepcode.com/products/rspec-basics">rSpec Basics</a> - A look at Behavior Driven Development. An ideal starting point if you haven't touched BDD yet but have been intrigued.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://peepcode.com/products/rails-from-scratch-part-i">Rails From Scratch: Part I</a> - A great up-to-date introductory "course" for getting into Rails. Even includes a free 20 page reference guide to Ruby and Rails, and an introduction to Ruby syntax. It's everything a new Rails developer needs.</li>
<li>
<a href="http://peepcode.com/products/rails-from-scratch-part-ii">Rails From Scratch: Part II</a> - Another 80 minutes of great introductory material for new Rails developers.</li>
</ul>
<p>While Railscasts' screencasts are totally free, Peepcode screencasts cost $9 each, but for the great packaging, length, and total depth of the material covered, they're still bargains!</p>
