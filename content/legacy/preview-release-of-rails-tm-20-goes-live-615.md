<p>David Heinemeier Hansson gives the Rails™ community its birthday and Christmas presents all at once with the <a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2007/9/30/rails-2-0-0-preview-release">announcement</a> of a preview release of Rails 2.0:</p>
<blockquote><p>Behold, behold, Rails 2.0 is almost here. But before we can slap on the final stamp, we’re going to pass through a couple of trial release phases. The first is this preview release, which allows you to sample the goodies in their almost finished state.</p></blockquote>
<p>The King <a href="http://weblog.rubyonrails.org/2007/9/30/rails-2-0-0-preview-release">goes into quite some depth</a> and covers a wide range of different and new features that have made it into Rails 2.0. </p>
<p>Keep in mind that this is just a <em>preview</em> release, so if you're not used to living on the edge, wait a little while until the release candidates or Rails 2.0 proper. In the meantime, check out <a href="http://pastie.caboo.se/private/krcevozww61drdeza13e3a">this small Ruby program</a> that checks to see if your app is already up to scratch for Rails 2.0.</p>
