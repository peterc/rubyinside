<p>This post just links to a few Ruby videos I've found on Google Video that I hadn't seen before.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-8103284744220333344&amp;q=ruby">How to Design A Domain Specific Language</a></strong><strong> with David Pollak.</strong> <em>1 hour 2 minutes.</em> Slides easy to read, camera is close to projector. David kicks things off with a surprising answer to "What is the world's most popular programming language?" that grabbed my attention straight away.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=1541014406319673545&amp;q=ruby">Code Generation With Ruby</a></strong><strong> with Jack Herrington.</strong> <em>50 minutes.</em> Interestingly, a product manager from Google suggests Ruby will "in the long run" become "more and more popular here", which is good.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=5225802147352970175&amp;q=ruby">Ruby and Google Maps</a></strong><strong> with Andre Lewis.</strong> <em>1 hour 2 minutes.</em> Unfortunately the resolution isn't good enough to see any of the code he's working on and the camera is fixed.</p>
