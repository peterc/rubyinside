<p><img src="/assets/2008/12/fukuoka.png" width="106" height="96" alt="fukuoka.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px; border:1px #000000 solid;">The Government of Fukuoka Japan, together with the Fukuoka Ruby Award Selection Committee, is running the <a href="http://www.ruby-award.jp/english.html">2009 Fukuoka Ruby Award Competition</a>. In short, they're trying to raise public awareness of the growing software industry in Japan but also Ruby in general.</p>
<p>With a deadline of Christmas Day, <strong>you only have just over 24 hours to apply!</strong> Two types of award are offered:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Award Type A is for a business model or system developed using Ruby.</p>
<p>Award Type B is for activities that contribute to the promotion, growth and expansion of the Ruby language.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The top prize is 1 million yen (approximately $11,114 at current rates) but three "outstanding performance award"s of 100,000 yen (approximately $1111) are also up for grabs. Basically, if you have a business model oriented around Ruby, have developed a Ruby application, or are otherwise helping to promote or grow the Ruby language, you should be good to go. Submissions are accepted via e-mail and the entry forms (in Excel format, alas) are <a href="http://www.ruby-award.jp/english.html">available from the Ruby Award site</a> along with all of the relevant terms and conditions. Entries must be sent by email by 11:59PM on Christmas Day, 2008 and the winners will be announced in February 2009.</p>
<p>At the very least, this is an opportunity to win a cash prize in return for rather little effort, so fill out an entry form and send it off before Christmas Day (Japan is 14-17 hours ahead of the US - so you need to play it safe).</p>
