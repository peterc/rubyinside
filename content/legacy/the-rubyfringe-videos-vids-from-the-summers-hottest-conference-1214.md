<p><img src="/assets/2008/09/rubyfringevids.jpg" width="137" height="38" alt="rubyfringevids.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"> Remember <a href="http://rubyfringe.com/">RubyFringe</a>, the avant-garde Ruby conference held in Canada this September? According to <a href="rubyfringe-success-and-roundup-956.html">most reports</a>, it went down as possible the best Ruby conference ever and spawned some very interesting presentations - that those of us who didn't go wouldn't have seen..</p>
<p>Luckily, in conjunction with InfoQ, videos from RubyFringe are making it online - so far there are four to check out:</p>
<div style="margin-left: 2em">
<p><a href="http://www.infoq.com/presentations/fernandez-sales-do-the-hustle"><strong>Obie Fernandez - Do The Hustle</strong></a> : Obie Fernandez of <a href="http://www.hashrocket.com/">HashRocket</a> looks at how to sell and price development services. How do you deal with proposals, contracts, RFPs? How do you close deals? It's all in here.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.infoq.com/presentations/john-lam-ruby-beyond-rails"><strong>John Lam - Ruby Beyond Rails</strong></a> : John Lam (developer of <a href="http://www.ironruby.net/">IronRuby</a> for Microsoft) talks about dynamic languages, and the challenges involved in developing IronRuby.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.infoq.com/interviews/sieger-jruby-warbler-rails"><strong>Nick Sieger Interview</strong></a> : Nick Sieger is quizzed on <a href="http://jruby.codehaus.org/">JRuby</a>, Java integration, and other Java / Ruby crossover projects.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.infoq.com/presentations/grigsby-deployment-monoculture"><strong>Dan Grigsby - Deployment Monoculture</strong></a> : Dan Grigsby on the concept of "trying out many different ideas before turning one into a startup." Dan is a very proactive and successful enterpreneur who has a lot of interesting things to say, particularly for those interested in building their own small, agile startups.</p>
</div>
