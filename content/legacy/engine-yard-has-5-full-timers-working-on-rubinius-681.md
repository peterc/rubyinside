<p><a href="http://weblog.raganwald.com/2007/12/why-rubinius-matters-to-rubys-future.html">Why Rubinius Matters to Ruby's Future</a> is a short blog post by Reg Braithwaite that explains why the <a href="http://rubini.us/">Rubinius</a> "Ruby in Ruby" implementation project is important to Ruby's future. In short, it's because Rubinius lets you tweak and add new features to the language at a low level using the Ruby language itself (well, a subset, but who's counting.. Captain Crunch?).</p>
<p>Ordinarily, such a short post, despite being good, wouldn't get a post to itself on the Ruby Inside front page, but a comment from Ezra Zygmuntowicz, of <a href="http://engineyard.com/%20">Engine Yard</a>, and a co-worker of Rubinius' founder, Evan Phoenix, got me little tip-tappers all over-excited:</p>
<blockquote><p>[...] we (engineyard.com) have hired a full time staff of 5 people to work solely on Rubinius. Expect a lot of progress over the next few months.</p></blockquote>
<p>Five full-time staff members on Rubinius? I'm sure the whole Ruby world can't wait to see the result of this in a few months' time!</p>
