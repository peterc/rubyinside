<p><img src="/assets/2008/04/euruko2008logo.jpg" width="277" height="111" alt="euruko2008logo.png"></p>
<p>As anyone who was present at this year's European Ruby Conference, <a href="http://www.euruko2008.org/">EURUKO 2008</a>, will attest: it was a great event! Over 300 Rubyists from all over Europe (and the world, with attendees from Japan, Australia and the US too) got together to talk, drink, and be merry.</p>
<p>The level of attendance came as a shock to the organizers, but they dealt with it excellently and put on a great event for all of us. Thanks must go to the CSRUG (<a href="http://www.csrug.cz/">Czechoslovakian Ruby Users' Group</a>) and its chairman Jiří Kubíček, along with Karel Minařík who played a big role. <a href="http://www.ff.cuni.cz/awelcome.php">The Faculty of Philosophy and Arts at Charles University, Prague</a>, also deserves a massive mention for providing such a great venue.</p>
<p>With a (very) minor role in helping to organize the conference, I post on Ruby Inside to thank the sponsors who helped make the event such a success. Their contributions enabled us all to get a pretty cool t-shirt (designed by Thomas Fuchs, of script.aculo.us fame), a Ruby notepad, and lots of other swag.</p>
<p><big><strong>Platinum Sponsor - <a href="http://www.sun.com/">Sun Microsystems</a></strong></big></p>
<p><img src="/assets/2008/04/sunlogo.jpg" width="97" height="49" alt="sunlogo.png"></p>
