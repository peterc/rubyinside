<p><img width="254" vspace="4" hspace="4" height="160" border="1" alt="Sitepointsurvey" src="/assets/2006/09/sitepointsurvey.png"><br>
Josh Catone <a href="http://www.railsforum.com/viewtopic.php?id=532">looks at the results</a> of SitePoint's <a href="http://www.sitepoint.com/report2006/">State of Web Development 2006 survey</a>. Josh says:</p>
<blockquote><p><em>According to respondents Ruby is used as a development platform by 5.31%, well behind PHP's dominating 67.54% market penetration.  However, when asked what platforms people were not currently developing for but planning to use in the next twelve months, Ruby was the answer of 24.37% of respondents!</em></p></blockquote>
<p><em><br>
</em>Of more immediate interest to me is that Python only takes 2.96% of the vote and Perl has 9.79% of the vote. And in response to "What server platform(s) are you <em>not</em> developing for, but expect to in the next 12 months?" Ruby takes 24.37% of the vote in comparison to just 8.47% for Python. Things aren't looking good for Python amongst SitePoint's developers!</p>
