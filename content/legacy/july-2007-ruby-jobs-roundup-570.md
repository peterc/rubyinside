<p>There are three jobs from the <a href="http://jobs.rubyinside.com/">Ruby Inside Job Board</a> from the last month:</p>
<p><strong>Rails Developer for Startup (San Francisco, CA)</strong><br>
A <a href="http://jobs.rubyinside.com/a/jbb/job-details/12048">Rails Developer or Senior Rails Developer is needed</a> at Affinity Labs, Inc, based in San Francisco, California. Affinity is a typical fast-moving startup, and they're looking for people especially with skills in areas like Ruby, Rails, ERB, AJAX, RJS, YAML, REST and Capistrano. Basically, it's a SF-based Rails developer's dream job! You can even pick which OS you work on, you get a fully stocked fridge, and bi-weekly happy hour parties.</p>
<p><strong>Rails Developer, System Integration Engineer, and Platform App Developer for Clinical Research Software Co (St. Louis, MO)</strong><br>
Medical Decision Logix, a St. Louis based medical software company, is <a href="http://jobs.rubyinside.com/a/jbb/job-details/12072">looking for three subtly different types of Ruby / Rails developers</a> who are ready to "help humanity!" Requirements for each of the three jobs are available at the link.</p>
<p><strong>Ruby / Rails Developers for ThoughtWorks (Atlanta, GA)</strong><br>
Thoughtworks, an "Agile" international development agency, is <a href="http://jobs.rubyinside.com/a/jbb/job-details/12080">looking for Ruby and Rails developers</a> to work on enterprise-level business applications. They provide a diverse, high energy environment, and are home to some of the best top-end developers.</p>
<p>If your company has openings for Ruby and/or Rails developers in any capacity, consider posting over at the Job Board. It costs $99 for 60 days of exposure, and each month I post the new jobs in a post like this for our 9500+ RSS subscribers to see. The jobs are also featured on the sidebar of every page of the RubyInside.com blog.</p>
