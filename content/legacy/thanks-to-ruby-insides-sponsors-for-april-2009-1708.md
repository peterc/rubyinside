<p>It's time to thank those great companies and individuals who help keep Ruby Inside (and often other Ruby sites) going.</p>
<p style="clear: left;"><big><strong>Big Nerd Ranch - Ruby and Rails Training</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.bignerdranch.com/"><img src="/assets/2009/04/bnr.png" width="153" height="57" alt="bnr.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.bignerdranch.com/">Big Nerd Ranch</a> offers intensive, head-down computer programming courses taught by experts in a retreat environment. Classroom, accommodations, and dining all take place within the same building, freeing you to learn, code, and discuss with your programming peers and instructors.</p>
<p>The current focus is on their <a href="http://bignerdranch.com/classes/ruby_on_rails.shtml">Ruby on Rails Bootcamp</a> taking place from June 8 to June 12. Charles B Quinn will be instructing. 5 full days of learning Rails in Atlanta, Georgia.</p>
<p style="clear: left;"><big><strong>JetBrains - Developer Tools and IDEs</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/"><img src="/assets/2009/04/jetbrains.png" width="154" height="66" alt="jetbrains.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/">JetBrains</a> is a renowned developer tools company, most famous for the Java IDE <a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/idea/index.html">IntelliJ IDEA</a>. Recently they <a href="rubymine-1-beta-intellij-ruby-and-rails-ide-1675.html">launched the beta of RubyMine 1.0</a>, a Ruby and Rails IDE they're currently developing. Initial reviews have been very positive.</p>
<p>To learn more about RubyMine, <a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/ruby/">click here</a>.</p>
<p style="clear: left;"><big><strong>Intridea - Rails Development and Application Design</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.intridea.com/"><img src="/assets/2009/04/intridea.png" width="157" height="59" alt="intridea.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.intridea.com/">Intridea™</a> is a full service web product and consulting firm based out of Washington, DC, specializing in enterprise 2.0 technology and collaboration tools with a focus on Ruby on Rails development. They have <a href="http://www.intridea.com/blog/development">a really cool development blog</a> that covers a lot of the work they do in the Ruby and Rails communities. Recent blog posts include <a href="http://www.intridea.com/2009/3/25/using-git-submodules-for-shared-rails-components?blog=development">Using Git Submodules for Shared Rails Components</a> and <a href="http://www.intridea.com/2009/3/23/twitter-auth-for-near-instant-twitter-apps?blog=development">TwitterAuth: For Near-Instant Twitter Apps</a>.</p>
<p style="clear: left;"><big><strong>Linode - Xen Virtual Servers</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.linode.com"><img src="/assets/2008/09/linode.jpg" width="153" height="47" alt="linode.png" style="float: left; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;"></a><a href="http://www.linode.com/">Linode</a> is a well-established provider of virtual private server hosting - some five years now. They're very competitive and have recently redesigned their site. Their entry level package is $19.95 and gives you 360MB of RAM, 12GB of storage, and 200GB bandwidth. I personally pay (no freebies!) for my own Linode to play around with and it costs $39.95 a month for 720MB of RAM and 24GB of space, which works really well with Phusion Passenger for running Rails apps (and <a href="http://www.sinatrarb.com/">Sinatra</a> apps, for that matter). A blog post <a href="http://journal.dedasys.com/2008/11/24/slicehost-vs-linode">Slicehost vs Linode</a> compares them to Slicehost - they stand up well!</p>
<p style="clear: left;"><big><strong>New Relic - Rails Performance Monitoring Systems</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.newrelic.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/09/newreliclogo.jpg" width="153" height="35" alt="newreliclogo.png" style="float: left; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px;"></a><a href="http://www.newrelic.com/">New Relic</a> provides high-end performance monitoring solutions for Rails applications and they include <a href="http://www.37signals.com">37signals</a> and <a href="http://www.shopify.com">Shopify</a> amongst their customers (very high praise indeed!) Recently New Relic <a href="http://blogs.newrelic.com/2008/11/youre-so-money.html">launched an affiliate program</a> (which you can still join), and has announced that almost 1000 customers now use RPM to manage their production Rails apps! Back in November, New Relic also <a href="http://blogs.newrelic.com/2008/11/new-relic-secures-60-million-in-new.html">secured $6m of new funding.</a></p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>Rubysophic - Ruby and Rails Performance and Diagnostics Tools</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.rubysophic.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/09/rubysophic.jpg" width="153" height="36" alt="rubysophic.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.rubysophic.com/">Rubysophic</a> is a new entrant to the Rails tools and service market. They're building diagnostics solutions for Ruby applications in general, in contrast to other players in the market who are Rails focused. <strong>Rubysophic's first product is</strong> <a href="http://www.rubysophic.com/products/RubyRun/"><strong>RubyRun</strong></a><strong>, a free stand-alone performance and diagnostics tool for both Ruby and Rails developers.</strong></p>

<p><a href="http://www.fynspire.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/07/fynspire-logo.jpg" width="148" height="69" alt="fynspire_logo.png" style="float: left; margin-top: 0px; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px; margin-left: 0px;"></a>San Francisco based <a href="http://www.fynspire.com/">Fynspire</a> are Rails development experts who specialize in social networking applications, widgets, and APIs. <p style="clear: left;"><big><strong>BrightBox - British Ruby on Rails Hosting</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.brightbox.co.uk/"><img src="/assets/2008/02/brightbox200802.jpg" width="153" height="53" alt="brightbox200802.png" style="float: left; margin-top: 0px; margin-right: 12px; margin-bottom: 12px; margin-left: 0px;"></a><a href="http://www.brightbox.co.uk/">BrightBox</a> is a British hosting company specializing in high-end Ruby on Rails application deployment. There's been a pent-up demand for European Rails hosting, and BrightBox offer a wide range of plans, from a basic VPS with 256MB of memory up to a 2GB-packed behemoth. The bandwidth allowances are pretty good by UK standards too, and all but the entry-level plan come with FiveRuns' <a href="http://www.fiveruns.com/products/manage">RM-Manage</a> technology.</p>
