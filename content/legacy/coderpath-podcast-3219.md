<p><img src="/assets/2010/04/coderpath.png" class="alignleft size-thumbnail bordered"><a href="http://coderpath.com">Coderpath</a> is a weekly podcast by Ruby developers Miles Forrest and Curtis McHale where they typically interview a different Ruby developer and discuss some of their current work. <strong>Most</strong> of the episodes are in an interview format and guests so far include a handful of Ruby developers you'll know (such as <span class="caps">DHH</span> and Ryan Bates).</p>
<p>The <a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/6">latest episode</a> is a 35 minute interview with Wayne E Seguin, the developer of <a href="rvm-ruby-version-manager-2347.html" title="RVM">Ruby Version Manager</a>. Before that was <a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/5">yours truly</a>, sounding like a dum-dum. Coming up next week is Obie Fernandez of <a href="http://www.hashrocket.com/">HashRocket</a>.</p>
<p>Here are the episodes so far:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/1">Episode 1: <span class="caps">DHH</span></a> (of <a href="http://www.railsinside.com/">Rails</a> fame)</li>
<li><a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/2">Episode 2: It's All About the Learning</a></li>
<li><a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/3">Episode 3: Off to MountainWest RubyConf</a></li>
<li>
<a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/4">Episode 4: Ryan Bates</a> (of <a href="http://railscasts.com/">RailsCasts</a> fame)</li>
<li>
<a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/5">Episode 5: Peter Cooper</a> (of dubious fame)</li>
<li><a href="http://coderpath.com/posts/6">Episode 6: Wayne E Seguin</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Naturally, you can <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/podcast/coderpath-podcast/id214162182">subscribe via iTunes</a> or use their <a href="http://feeds.feedburner.com/coderpath">direct podcast feed</a> with your favorite podcatching software. You can also <a href="http://twitter.com/coderpath">follow the podcast on Twitter</a>, which is useful because they often request questions to ask people before recording the show.</p>
