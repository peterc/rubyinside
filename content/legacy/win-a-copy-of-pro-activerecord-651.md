<p><a href="http://www.apress.com/book/view/9781590598474">Pro Active Record</a> "Databases with Ruby and Rails" (<a href="http://www.amazon.com/Pro-Active-Record-Databases-Rails/dp/1590598474">Amazon</a>) is a new book by Kevin Marshall, Chad Pytel, and Jon Yurek, published by Apress. The book goes deep into the <a href="http://wiki.rubyonrails.org/rails/pages/ActiveRecord">ActiveRecord</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Object-relational_mapping">ORM</a> library, a key part of Rails that makes it easy to deal with information stored within a database in an object oriented way. There are already <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Pro-Active-Record-Databases-Rails/dp/1590598474">several reviews on Amazon.com</a>, although Josh Susser has written <a href="http://blog.hasmanythrough.com/2007/10/29/book-review-pro-active-record">something more indepth</a>.</p>
<p>To celebrate the launch of the book, the guys over at ThoughtBot (where both Chad and Jon work) are <a href="http://giantrobots.thoughtbot.com/2007/11/13/the-contest">running a contest</a> with three prizes of signed copies of Pro Active Record. To enter, you have to e-mail ThoughtBot with examples of the craziest and most absurd application ideas your clients have come up with. Some examples and details of how to enter are in ThoughtBot's <a href="http://giantrobots.thoughtbot.com/2007/11/13/the-contest">blog post</a>. Good luck!</p>
