<p><img align="right" alt="Joyent Slingshot Logo" src="/assets/2007/05/joyent-slingshot-logo.png"><a href="http://developers.joyent.com/">Joyent Slingshot</a> allows developers to deploy Rails applications that work the same online and offline (with synchronization) and with <a href="http://developers.joyent.com/wiki/DesktopIntegration">drag into and out of the application</a> just like a standard desktop application. Check its <a href="http://developers.joyent.com/wiki/BasicApp">two-minutes tutorial</a> to get a feel of how you can make it work.</p>
<p>Earlier this month, <a href="http://joyeur.com/2007/05/01/slingshot-public-release">they finally released Slingshot</a> to the public. They also celebrated it with a contest offering their $1250-worth <a href="http://www.joyent.com/accelerator">Accelerator</a> for the prize.</p>
<p>We've <a href="build-offline-rails-applications-with-joyent-slingshot-435.html">posted</a> about Slingshot before it was officially released. In addition to stabilizing the overall framework, they also have made several enhancements, including the decision that they will <a href="http://joyeur.com/2007/04/17/joyent-slingshot-business-model">open source</a> the technology (planned for June).</p>
<p>You can find more information on their <a href="http://developers.joyent.com/">developer wiki</a>.</p>
