<p>There have been a bunch of interview related links coming in over the past week, so it makes sense to put them all together to give you a nice pre-Christmas injection of interesting interviews to read. Enjoy!</p>
<p><strong>Why Several Ruby Experts Are In Love With Ruby/Rails</strong></p>
<p>Job search site <a href="http://www.odinjobs.com/">OdinJobs</a> <a href="http://www.odinjobs.com/blogs/careers/entry/ruby_experts_whythey_are_in">interviewed several "Ruby experts"</a> (and I only use quotes there because I'm in the list!) and asked them why they like Ruby and Rails and for tidbits of advice to newcomers to the Ruby community. The experts are Satish Talim, Alex Wayne, Alex Leverington, Abhay Kumar, Adam Williams, Alex Kane, Adam Keys, and Peter Cooper.</p>
<p><strong>Peter Cooper (Me!)</strong></p>
<p>Larry Wright took the opportunity to <a href="http://approachingnormal.com/2007/12/19/interview-peter-cooper-on-rails-and-entrepeneurship">interview me</a>, Peter Cooper of Ruby Inside, and asked me about what I get up to, how I got into Ruby and Rails, what sort of tools I use in my work, and my opinions on JRuby.</p>
<p><strong>Topher Cyll - Author of Practical Ruby Projects</strong></p>
<p>Intrepid interviewer Pat Eyler (of <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/">On Ruby</a>) has <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/12/interview-with-topher-cyll-author-of.html">interviewed Topher Cyll</a>, the author of Practical Ruby Projects, published by Apress. Pat and Topher basically stick to the topic of the book, including how the contents were chosen and what Topher plans to do next.</p>
<p><strong>Participants of Satish Talim's online Ruby class</strong></p>
<p>Pat Eyler <a href="http://on-ruby.blogspot.com/2007/12/satish-talimsnew-ruby-class-and.html">interviews three participants</a> of Satish Talim's fine <a href="http://rubylearning.com/blog/">online Ruby classes</a>: Marcos Souza, Chris Porter, and Michael Uplawski. They talk about how the class works and cover some things that the beginning Ruby developers found interesting about the language.</p>
