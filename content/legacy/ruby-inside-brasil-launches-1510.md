<p><a href="http://www.rubyinside.com.br/"><img src="/assets/2009/02/rubyinsidebrasil.png" width="134" height="105" alt="rubyinsidebrasil.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a> <em>Bom dia</em> to all of Ruby Inside's Brazilian (and Portuguese) readers! You can now, if you prefer, read (and subscribe to) the Portuguese language edition of Ruby Inside at <a href="http://www.rubyinside.com.br/"><strong>http://www.rubyinside.com.br/</strong></a> <strong><span style="font-weight: normal;"><em>(if you'd rather just subscribe to the feed, it's</em> <a href="http://feeds2.feedburner.com/RubyInsideBrasil"><em>here</em></a><em>, or check it out on Twitter</em> <a href="http://twitter.com/rubyinside_br"><em>@rubyinside_br</em></a><em>)</em></span></strong></p>
<p><a href="http://www.rubyinside.com.br/"><strong>Ruby Inside Brasil</strong></a> <strong>(Brazil)</strong> is an initial step into localizing Ruby Inside by "franchising" to trusted editors and translators in different parts of the world (there will also be unique content over time). Ruby Inside Brasil's editor is <strong>Carlos Brando</strong> of <a href="http://surgeworks.com/">Surgeworks, Inc</a>, who is well known in the Brazilian Rails community for his blog <a href="http://www.nomedojogo.com/">Nome do Jogo</a>. He also has the support of <a href="http://twitter.com/EliezerPimentel">Eliézer Pimentel</a>, José Gomes, Levy Carneiro and Rafael Rosa and <a href="http://twitter.com/luisbebop">Luís Gustavo</a> to translate posts from Ruby Inside and <a href="http://www.railsinside.com/">Rails Inside</a>. Naturally, readers in Portugal (and Macau, Angola, and other places) will also benefit thanks to the shared language.</p>
<p>Carlos has done a great job by already securing his first sponsor, <a href="http://www.planobe.com.br/">Plano Bê</a>, a Brazilian software development company that works in the electronic transactions market. It's still very early days for the Ruby Inside localization experiment, but I wish Carlos all the best for being the first to give it a go.</p>
