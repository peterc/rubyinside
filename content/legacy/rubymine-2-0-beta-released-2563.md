<p><img src="/assets/2009/09/rubymine2.png" width="172" height="111" alt="rubymine2.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"><a href="rubymine-a-new-intelligent-ruby-ide-built-on-intellj-1302.html">RubyMine</a> is a Ruby and Rails IDE (for Windows, OS X, <i>and</i> Linux) by <a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/index.html">JetBrains</a>, the guys behind the popular Java IDE <a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/idea/index.html">IntelliJ IDEA</a>. We've previously posted about <a href="rubymine-1-0-ruby-ide-1818.html">how much people seem to like RubyMine</a>, and it looks like things will get even better, as they've just released <a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/ruby/nextversion/index.html">the beta of RubyMine 2.0</a>. Notably, RubyMine 2.0 will be free to existing 1.0 users as it falls within the year allowed for free updates!</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14px; font-weight: bold;">The New Stuff</span></p>
<p>RubyMine 2.0 was initially meant to only be RubyMine <i>1.5</i> but Eugene Toporov of JetBrains told me that they've considered 2.0 to be more appropriate given the number of updates and additions. Updates include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Ruby 1.9 support</li>
<li>Rails 2.3.3 support</li>
<li>Rails i18n (internationalization) support and spell checker</li>
<li>UI improvements</li>
<li>Cucumber and Shoulda support (in addition to the existing, and now improved, RSpec support)</li>
<li>Built-in HAML and Sass support</li>
</ul>
<p>JetBrains' <a href="http://www.jetbrains.com/ruby/nextversion/index.html">RubyMine 2.0 beta overview page</a> gives full examples of the changes and improvements.</p>
<h3>20% Discount Until General Availability</h3>
<p>JetBrains are offering a 20% discount on purchases of RubyMine from now until the general release of 2.0 - due sometime in the next few weeks, so this is a great time to try it out and, if you like it, buy a copy. The offer price is currently $79 to US users or £57 in the UK. Not bad considering the parent product, IntelliJ IDEA, weighs in at hundreds of dollars!</p>
<h3>Still A Bit Flaky To Me, But A Strong Step Forward</h3>
<p>I'm not typically into IDEs and find the lack of a truly <i>native</i> OS X Ruby IDE a little jarring. RubyMine 2.0 does seem to have a somewhat improved interface, though, but I still find it flaky. For example, when you press backspace when filling out fields in the preferences dialogs, it doesn't work, and instead you see characters being deleted from your open code in the background! When filling out other dialog boxes, when I pressed Enter it added lines into my code in the background too rather than close the dialog.</p>
<p>RubyMine isn't a native OS X app from what I can tell (you can reskin it from the preferences pane!) and dialog and UI behavior is not really up to OS X standard in much of the app. Despite reservations about using it myself, though, other <a href="http://search.twitter.com/search?q=rubymine">users on Twitter are raving about how good it is</a> so it's certainly worth a try. It's certainly improved enough that I'm going to give it a proper try this time.</p>
