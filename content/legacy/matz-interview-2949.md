<p><img src="/assets/2010/01/its-matz-baby.png" width="155" height="111" alt="its-matz-baby.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px; border:1px #000000 solid;">Ruby's creator and benevolent dictator Yukihiro "Matz" Matsumoto has <a href="http://www.infoq.com/interviews/yukihiro-matz-language-design">done a video interview for InfoQ</a> at the QCon enterprise software development conference. You can watch the video on <a href="http://www.infoq.com/interviews/yukihiro-matz-language-design">InfoQ's page</a> (or, if you're a member of InfoQ, download an MP3).</p>
<p>In the interview, Matz talks about what he'd do if he were recreated Ruby from scratch today, lazy evaluation, Erlang, actor models, typing, his opinions on alternative Ruby implementations, and continuations.</p>
<p>Matz also recommends reading O'Reilly's <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Beautiful-Code-Leading-Programmers-Practice/dp/0596510047?tag=rubins-20"><i>Beautiful Code</i></a> (Amazon link) but humbly advises against reading Chapter 29 - a chapter that he contributed to the book.</p>
<p style="background-color:#ffd;padding:8px;font-family:verdana;font-size:12px"><em>[jobs]</em> The <a href="http://jobs.rubyinside.com/a/jbb/find-jobs">Ruby Inside jobs board</a> has blown up this month with 14 jobs currently active. They span from New York to Chicago, Akron, Birmingham, and Portland, so check them out if you're looking for something new to do.</p>
