<p>It's time to thank those great companies and individuals who help keep Ruby Inside (and often other Ruby sites) going. <em>Note: All descriptions and notes are written by Ruby Inside and are not directly influenced by the sponsors. As such, any opinions stated are those of Ruby Inside and not necessarily shared by the sponsor!</em></p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>New Relic - Rails Performance Monitoring Systems</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.newrelic.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/09/newreliclogo.jpg" width="153" height="35" alt="newreliclogo.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.newrelic.com/">New Relic</a> provides high-end performance monitoring solutions for Rails applications and they include <a href="http://www.37signals.com">37signals</a> and <a href="http://www.shopify.com">Shopify</a> amongst their customers (very high praise indeed!) Recently New Relic <a href="http://blogs.newrelic.com/2008/11/youre-so-money.html">launched an affiliate program</a> (which you can still join), and has announced that almost 1000 customers now use RPM to manage their production Rails apps! In November, New Relic also <a href="http://blogs.newrelic.com/2008/11/new-relic-secures-60-million-in-new.html">secured $6m of new funding.</a></p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>FiveRuns - Rails Application Monitoring Solutions</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.fiveruns.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/03/fiveruns-310by90.jpg" width="149" height="43" alt="fiveruns_310by90.gif" style="float: left; margin: 0px 12px 12px 0px"></a><a href="http://www.fiveruns.com/">FiveRuns</a> is a provider of Rails application (and server) monitoring services. FiveRuns works hard to be part of the community, whether by releasing <a href="http://www.fiveruns.com/products/install">a free Ruby and Rails stack</a> or publishing a <a href="take-five-rails-interviews-767.html">great set of interviews</a> with Ruby developers on their blog. Just two days ago they published an extensive and interesting <a href="http://blog.fiveruns.com/2008/12/1/rails-takefive-conversation-with-glenn-vanderburg">interview between Adam Keys and Glenn Vanderburg.</a></p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>BrightBox - British Ruby on Rails Hosting</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.brightbox.co.uk/"><img src="/assets/2008/02/brightbox200802.jpg" width="153" height="53" alt="brightbox200802.png" style="float: left; margin: 0px 12px 12px 0px"></a><a href="http://www.brightbox.co.uk/">BrightBox</a> is a British hosting company specializing in high-end Ruby on Rails application deployment. There's been a pent-up demand for European Rails hosting, and BrightBox offer a wide range of plans, from a basic VPS with 256MB of memory up to a 2GB-packed behemoth. The bandwidth allowances are pretty good by UK standards too, and all but the entry-level plan come with FiveRuns' <a href="http://www.fiveruns.com/products/manage">RM-Manage</a> technology.</p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>Linode - Xen Virtual Servers</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.linode.com"><img src="/assets/2008/09/linode.jpg" width="153" height="47" alt="linode.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.linode.com/">Linode</a> is a well-established provider of virtual private server hosting - some five years now. They're very competitive and have recently redesigned their site. Their entry level package is $19.95 and gives you 360MB of RAM, 12GB of storage, and 200GB bandwidth. I personally pay (no freebies!) for my own Linode to play around with and it costs $39.95 a month for 720MB of RAM and 24GB of space, which works really well with Phusion Passenger for running Rails apps. A blog post <a href="http://journal.dedasys.com/2008/11/24/slicehost-vs-linode">Slicehost vs Linode</a> compares them to Slicehost - they stand up well!</p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>Atlantic Dominion Solutions - Rails Consultancy and Development</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.techcfl.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/02/atlds200802.jpg" width="151" height="53" alt="atlds200802.png" style="float: left; margin: 0px 12px 12px 0px"></a>Based in Orlando, Florida, <a href="http://www.techcfl.com/">Atlantic Dominion Solutions</a> offer a full range of Rails consultancy and development services. Founder Robert Dempsey is also behind the <a href="http://www.railsforall.org/">Rails For All</a> project, a Rails advocacy group. ADS's latest release is <a href="http://www.expensd.com/">Expensd</a>, a Web application that simplifies expense tracking.</p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>PeepCode - Rails Screencasts and PDF E-Books</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.peepcode.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/02/peepcode200802.jpg" width="154" height="70" alt="peepcode200802.png" style="float: left; margin: 0px 12px 12px 0px"></a><a href="http://www.peepcode.com">PeepCode</a> has been the longest-term sponsor of Ruby Inside and we all love Mr Geoffrey Grosenbach and his growing team of authors and contributed for all they give to our community.</p>
<p><strong><em>New!</em></strong> Peepcode's latest release is <a href="http://peepcode.com/products/productivity-for-programmers">Productivity for Programmers by Lars Pind</a>.</p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>eSpace - Rails Scaling Specialists</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.espace.com.eg/"><img src="/assets/2008/09/espace.jpg" width="155" height="61" alt="espace.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.espace.com.eg/">eSpace</a> is an Egyptian Rails development company that specializes in the scaling of Rails applications. They've recently hit the headlines in the Ruby and Rails communities thanks to their development of <a href="http://www.espace.com.eg/neverblock">NeverBlock</a> and <a href="mysqlplus-non-blocking-mysql-driver-for-ruby-1138.html">MySQLPlus</a>.</p>
<p>eSpace are also offering a <a href="http://www.espace.com.eg/services/swat">SWAT (Scalable Web Applications Team)</a> service. This is a service where they can partner up with you to help solve your scalability issues. See - Rails CAN scale!</p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>Fynspire - Social Network App Development Experts</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.fynspire.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/07/fynspire-logo.jpg" width="148" height="69" alt="fynspire_logo.png" style="float: left; margin: 0px 12px 12px 0px"></a> San Francisco based <a href="http://www.fynspire.com/">Fynspire</a> are Rails development experts who specialize in social networking applications, widgets, and APIs. Their latest creation is the <a href="http://www.gettingpopular.com/">Getting Popular</a> social networking tool.</p>
<p>If you need a social networking application developed, get in touch with these guys now.</p>
<p style="clear: left"><big><strong>Rubysophic - Ruby and Rails Performance and Diagnostics Tools</strong></big></p>
<p><a href="http://www.rubysophic.com/"><img src="/assets/2008/09/rubysophic.jpg" width="153" height="36" alt="rubysophic.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"></a><a href="http://www.rubysophic.com/">Rubysophic</a> is a new entrant to the Rails tools and service market. They're building diagnostics solutions for Ruby applications in general, in contrast to other players in the market who are Rails focused. <strong>Rubysophic's first product is</strong> <a href="http://www.rubysophic.com/products/RubyRun/"><strong>RubyRun</strong></a><strong>, a free stand-alone performance and diagnostics tool for both Ruby and Rails developers.</strong></p>
