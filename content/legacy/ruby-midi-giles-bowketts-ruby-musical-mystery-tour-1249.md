<p><img src="/assets/2008/10/singalong.png" width="154" height="135" alt="singalong.png" style="float:left; margin-right:12px; margin-bottom:12px;"><a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/">Giles Bowkett</a> is anything but a quiet chap - indeed, he's one of the more <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2007/03/unit-testing-fizzbuzz-are-you-out-of.html">outspoken</a> members of our community. With the roar, however, comes a lot of wisdom, and Giles recent work on integrating Ruby and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musical_Instrument_Digital_Interface">MIDI</a> is inspired.</p>
<p>In July, Giles attended the RubyFringe conference and gave a talk with the unpronounceable title of "<a href="http://www.infoq.com/presentations/archaeopteryx-bowkett">Archaeopteryx: A Ruby MIDI Generator</a>", named after his <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2008/02/archaeopteryx-ruby-midi-generator.html">Archaeopteryx project</a>. This talk has been <em>very</em> well received generally. Pete Forde of Unspace <a href="http://rethink.unspace.ca/2008/10/2/have-you-watched-giles-bowkett-yet">called it</a> "<em>the most important video of 2008</em>", Pete Yandell <a href="http://twitter.com/notahat/statuses/953647585">said</a> it's the "<em>must-watch geek talk of the year</em>" and *** **** <a href="http://www.*******.com/conferences/rubyfringe_2008.html">said</a> Giles "<em>Ruled The World.</em>" It's an awesome talk, even if music and MIDI don't interest you. Giles covers building an ecosystem around your projects very well - if you're feeling uninspired, give it a look.</p>
<p>If you <em>are</em> interested in music and MIDI, however, start digging in to <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2008/02/archaeopteryx-ruby-midi-generator.html">Archaeopteryx</a> right away. It's billed as a "Ruby MIDI generator" but as of this week it <a href="http://gilesbowkett.blogspot.com/2008/10/archaeopteryx-midi-in.html">now also accepts MIDI in</a>. Awesomeness ensues.</p>
