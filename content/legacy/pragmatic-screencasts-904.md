<p>The <a href="http://www.pragprog.com">Pragmatic Programmers</a> (who brought us the "<a href="http://www.pragprog.com/titles/ruby/programming-ruby">Pickaxe</a>") have decided to branch into screencasting with <a href="http://www.pragprog.com/screencasts">Pragmatic Screencasts</a>. At launch, screencasts for Expression Engine, OS X Core Animation, Erlang, and Rails are available. On the Rails front, Ryan Bates (of <a href="http://railscasts.com/">Railscasts</a> fame) has been brought on board to create a series called "<a href="http://www.pragprog.com/screencasts/v-rbar/everyday-active-record">Everyday Active Record.</a>" So far two episodes, each focusing on a different area of Rails / Active Record, are available (at $5 each) but more are promised over time.</p>
<p>The first two episodes are "<span style="font-style: italic;">Designing Models with Associations</span>" and "<span style="font-style: italic;">Finding and Scoping Models.</span>" I've watched both and they do a great job of taking a chunk of Active Record / Rails functionality and demonstrating the "right" way to use it. Ryan's vast experience makes the screencasts good demonstrations of how to use Rails "properly."</p>
<p>The screencasts are certainly not for anybody who follows Rails Edge like a hawk and keeps on top of everything Rails, but for those who'd like to gain extra confidence from seeing a Rails master at work with Rails 2.1's features, they're a bargain. They're well produced, go along at a nice pace, and Ryan makes a good narrator. I look forward to seeing more from this series in future. Definitely consider checking them out, especially if you want to support Ryan for the hard work he does with <a href="http://railscasts.com/">Railscasts</a>.</p>
<p>It's also worth noting that the Pragmatic Programmers are also <a href="http://www.pragprog.com/frequently-asked-questions/screencasts">looking for other people</a> <em>(see bottom question)</em> who might be interested in working with them to produce more screencasts. They do the post-production and pay 50% royalties.</p>
