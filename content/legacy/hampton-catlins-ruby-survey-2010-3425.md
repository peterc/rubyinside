<blockquote class="stylized"><p>I finally got off my ass and got the results of Ruby Survey 2008/2009 online. Some really interesting stuff in there! And, along with releasing that information, I'm launching the 2010 survey with more questions about testing frameworks and etc.</p></blockquote>
<p><cite>Hampton Catlin</cite></p>
<p>For the past couple of years, Hampton Catlin (best known as the inventor of <a href="http://haml-lang.com/">Haml</a>) has run an annual <a href="http://survey.hamptoncatlin.com/">Ruby survey</a> and you can <a href="http://survey.hamptoncatlin.com/">take the 2010 survey now.</a> There are 23 multiple choice questions, mostly about your library and coding preferences.</p>
<p>At the time of posting, 3619 people have taken the survey and, interestingly, you can check out the results of the survey as it goes on - <a href="http://survey.hamptoncatlin.com/survey/stats">the results page</a> is automatically updated each day.</p>
<p>Things that stand out in the results are the significant growth in jQuery, Ruby 1.9 (up to 24% this year from 7.6% in 2009), and Haml usage, and that 10% of respondents list Sinatra as their webapp framework of choice (up from 2.8% in 2008).</p>
