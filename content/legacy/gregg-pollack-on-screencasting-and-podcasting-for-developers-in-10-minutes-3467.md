<p>I've been itching to do some audio or video interviews on Ruby Inside - here's the first! Gregg Pollack of <a href="http://envylabs.com/">EnvyLabs</a> and <a href="http://ruby5.envylabs.com/">Ruby5</a> (previously of RailsEnvy fame) is doing a screencasting and podcasting workshop at <a href="http://bizconf.org/?coupon=GPOLL">BizConf</a> so I thought I'd ask him what that entails and how developers can benefit from learning about these media. (I like things short and simple so the interview is <strong>just 10 minutes long.</strong>)</p>

<p><em><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4THEWkQ-BxA">Link to YouTube</a> in case the video embed doesn't work for you.</em></p>
<p>Products mentioned by Gregg include <a href="http://screenr.com/">Screenr</a>, <a href="http://jingproject.com/">Jing</a>, <a href="http://www.telestream.net/screen-flow">ScreenFlow</a>, and <a href="http://www.techsmith.com/camtasiamac/">Camtasia for Mac.</a> Gregg is also keen for you to get in touch with him if you have anything to promote on <a href="http://ruby5.envylabs.com/">the Ruby5 podcast.</a></p>
