<p><img src="/assets/2009/10/ruby-inside-design-transition.gif" width="600" height="228" alt="ruby-inside-design-transition.gif">They've had several days to settle in, but because most of you will be reading via the RSS feed it's time to make a point to announce that... <a href="index.html"><b>Ruby Inside</b></a> <b>and</b> <a href="http://www.railsinside.com"><b>Rails Inside</b></a> <b>have both been redesigned!</b> If you usually just read the full-text RSS feed, do come and visit the site - if only to let us know what you think.</p>
<p>The new design marks the 3rd major redevelopment of Ruby Inside since 2006 and the stats from the last week are showing it to be a lot more effective with a 40% increase in pageviews per visitor. As well as an improved design, new features include:</p>
<ul>
<li>An extended "elsewhere" section with automatically updated links to other top Ruby content each day</li>
<li>Our listing of the "<b>Top 10 Ruby Sites</b>" on every page's sidebar (Wanna know what they are? <a href="index.html">Visit the site!</a>)</li>
<li>A listing of our recommended <b>Ruby books</b> on every sidebar</li>
<li>New, graphic-oriented "<b>related posts</b>" bar on every post - a great way to find other posts to read</li>
<li>
<b>Search</b> (people were asking for this a lot)</li>
<li>A "fixed"/permanent header bar - reaction so far has been positive but I'm not entirely sure it's quite right yet..</li>
<li>A <a href="post-a-job">proper page for people who want to post to our jobs board.</a> No longer are you dumped right into a sales form - you get to learn a bit about it first.</li>
</ul>
<p>Lastly, I'd like to thank all of you for reading and supporting us over the years - ultimately it's you, by reading every day, that keeps this site going.</p>
<p><em>As an aside, I've also quietly launched a <a href="http://therubyway.org/">The Ruby Way blog</a>, dedicated to unearthing interesting bits and pieces from Hal Fulton's awesome Ruby book.</em></p>
