<p>It was about a year ago now that Rubyist extraordinaire Why The Lucky Stiff first showed his "<a href="http://code.whytheluckystiff.net/shoes/">Shoes</a>" Ruby cross-platform GUI application toolkit off to the world. Since then, he's not let up with the development.</p>
<p>Previously, we've <a href="shoes-new-shoebox-and-nobody-knows-shoes-790.html">featured the release</a> of <a href="http://www.the-shoebox.org/">The Shoebox</a> - a Shoes application repository - and <a href="http://hackety.org/press/">Nobody Knows Shoes</a> - a short book about Shoes, done <a href="shoes-roundup-ruby-gui-app-development-goodness-597.html">a roundup of Shoes tutorials</a>, and looked at <a href="how-to-build-a-drawing-application-using-shoes-os-x-and-ruby-645.html">how to write a drawing program with Shoes</a> on OS X.</p>
<p>Now, Shoes has <a href="http://shoooes.net/">a new official homepage</a> - located at <a href="http://shoooes.net/">http://shoooes.net/</a> (yes, that's three <em>o</em>s). Complete with a picture of an Amstrad CPC and gloriously pixellated logo, there's now a reasonably non-technical place to go to download Shoes, get into the wiki, and get access to the book. There's also <a href="http://shoooes.net/tutorial/">a really good illustrative tutorial</a> that walks you through some basic Shoes examples.</p>
<p>Quit dallying - go <a href="http://shoooes.net/">check it out</a>. It's quite possibly Why's best work yet.</p>
