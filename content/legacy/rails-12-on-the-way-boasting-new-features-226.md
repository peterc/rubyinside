<p>Bob Silva presents a <a href="http://www.railtie.net/articles/2006/09/05/rush-to-rails-1-2-adds-tons-of-new-features">rundown of some new features due in Rails 1.2</a>. These minor features are in addition to the full <a href="http://www.loudthinking.com/arc/000593.html">REST support</a> <strike>and <a href="http://ryandaigle.com/articles/2006/06/30/whats-new-in-edge-rails-activeresource-is-here">ActiveResource</a></strike> (<em>supposedly not just yet</em>), as previously covered by a multitude of blogs.</p>
<p>David Heinemeier Hansson says Rails 1.2 won't be released before RailsConf Europe (in a couple of weeks' time) but a Release Candidate <em>may</em> be available. Judging from previous releases, I'd personally speculate we could expect Rails 1.2 to be released in mid October, but don't hold me to that.</p>
