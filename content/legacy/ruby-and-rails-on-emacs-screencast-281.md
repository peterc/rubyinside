<p>Those of us who primarily develop Rails applications on OS X are mostly familiar with <a href="http://www.macromates.com/">TextMate</a>, a great, powerful and stylish editor that seemingly entices more Rails developers over to the Mac platform every day (judging by the comments on the #rubyonrails IRC channel). However, another option available to all platforms is the GNU Emacs editor, an open source Emacs-based text editor that's been around since the computer dark ages.</p>
<p>Marshall Vandegrift has put together <a href="http://platypope.org/yada/emacs-demo/">a compelling screencast of his Emacs setup</a> which sports as many, if not more, features as TextMate, and is just as powerful. He has syntax coloring for Ruby and <em>erb</em>, a project view, and built-in links to tools such as <em>svn</em> and <em>diff</em>. He also blogs about Emacs and Ruby <a href="http://platypope.org/blog">here</a>.</p>
