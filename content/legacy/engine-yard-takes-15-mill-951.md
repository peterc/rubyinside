<p><a href="http://www.engineyard.com/">Engine Yard</a>, probably the first major Rails-focused hosting company, has today <a href="http://blog.engineyard.com/2008/7/14/engine-yard-closes-15-million-in-series-b-financing">taken a second round of finance of $15m</a>. This second round, from Benchmark Capital, Amazon.com, and New Enterprise Associates, follows on from <a href="rails-web-host-engine-yard-now-35-million-richer-694.html">January's $3.5m</a> from Benchmark.</p>
<p>So where will the money go? Ezra Zygmuntowicz <a href="http://brainspl.at/articles/2008/07/14/engine-yard-takes-15mill-series-b-round-from-nea-amazon-and-benchmark">says</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We’re going to use this money towards making Ruby the platform of choice for cloud computing and web development in startups and the enterprise alike.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>This assertion is without doubt, as Engine Yard continues to fund and support the development of <a href="http://www.rubini.us/">Rubinius</a> and <a href="http://www.merbivore.com/">Merb</a>, and supports several popular Ruby and Rails sites with sponsorships. A $15m round for Engine Yard also helps validates the business models surrounding Rails, in a similar way to <a href="new-relic-a-new-35-million-funded-player-in-the-rails-application-monitoring-space-880.html">New Relic's $3.5m funding round</a> in May.</p>
<p>Personally I am convinced that a significant part of Engine Yard's strategy will involve spending the cash on <a href="http://www.redbull.com/">Red Bull</a> in order to keep Engine Yard operating more quickly than the competition. At $2.50 a can, and the current complement of 80 employees, this works out at 75,000 cans of the amber nectar each. At a work-inducing 8 cans each work day, we're looking at about 37 years' worth of Red Bull to keep Engine Yard ahead of the pack.</p>
<p>To close, Ezra has shared some initial info regarding a new Engine Yard project called Vertebra that's worth keeping a look out for:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We’ve also been delving into the cloud computing arena as I think the next 5 years are going to see huge transition from standard hosting models into the cloud. Our upcoming Vertebra project is a new application programming platform for building distributed cloud applications with XMPP. You can expect to see the first open source release of Vertebra this summer, I think this is a truly unique and very fun project to work on.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Let's hope Engine Yard's newly acquired cashpile will be enough to get them through the recession and become highly profitable as this area takes off.</p>
<p><em>(Semi-disclaimer: Engine Yard sponsors <a href="http://www.rubyflow.com/">RubyFlow</a>, a sister site of Ruby Inside.)</em></p>
